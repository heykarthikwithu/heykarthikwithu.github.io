Bhagwatikumar Sharma

On3rd Apr 2025, 2025-11-03T12:09:27+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Bhagwatikumar Sharma was a renowned Gujarati writer, journalist, and poet, celebrated for his profound storytelling, realistic characters, and deep social consciousness. His contribution to Gujarati literature and journalism spanned several decades, making him one of the most influential literary figures of his time.

Early Life and Education

  • Born on May 31, 1934, in Surat, Gujarat, Bhagwatikumar Sharma grew up with a passion for literature.
  • His early education in Surat laid the foundation for his love for language, culture, and storytelling.
  • He was deeply influenced by the freedom struggle, post-independence socio-political changes, and the evolution of Gujarati literature.
  • Though he was initially drawn to science, he later shifted his focus to literature and journalism, where he made a significant impact.

Literary Contributions

Bhagwatikumar Sharma authored more than 80 books, including novels, short story collections, essays, and poetry. His works often focused on human relationships, social changes, and the struggles of the middle class. Some of his most acclaimed novels include:

  • "Asooryalok" – A critically acclaimed novel that delves into social and moral dilemmas
  • "Vyaktamadhya" – A story exploring complex human emotions and relationships
  • "Chhevatnu Chhevat" – A thought-provoking novel about existence and survival
  • "Samaydvip" – A historical and philosophical exploration of time and society

His stories were known for their deep psychological insight, lyrical prose, and emotional depth. His themes often revolved around urban life, migration, industrialization, and the evolving social fabric of Gujarat.

Role in Journalism

  • Apart from being a writer, Bhagwatikumar Sharma was an eminent journalist.
  • He served as the editor of Gujarat Mitra, one of the oldest newspapers in Gujarat.
  • His editorial writings reflected his sharp intellect, deep cultural understanding, and commitment to truth.
  • His contributions to journalism were not just limited to reporting; he played a crucial role in shaping public opinion and addressing pressing social and political issues through his insightful articles.

Awards and Recognition

For his remarkable literary contributions, Bhagwatikumar Sharma received numerous prestigious awards, including:

  • Sahitya Akademi Award (1988) for his novel Asooryalok
  • Ranjitram Suvarna Chandrak (1984), one of the highest literary honors in Gujarati literature
  • Narmad Suvarna Chandrak (1977) for his excellence in writing
  • Vrajlal K. Mehta Award for his overall contribution to Gujarati literature

Summary

Bhagwatikumar Sharma was a literary giant whose works captured the essence of Gujarat’s cultural, social, and emotional landscape. His novels, essays, and journalistic writings not only entertained but also enlightened readers about real-life issues. His rich legacy in Gujarati literature continues to inspire new generations of writers and readers alike.
