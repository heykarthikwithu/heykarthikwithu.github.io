A. P. Venkateswaran

Listen Pause Resume Stop

A. P. Venkateswaran (1930–2014) was an esteemed Indian diplomat and civil servant who served as the Foreign Secretary of India. Renowned for his intellect, straightforwardness, and unwavering dedication to his duties, Venkateswaran was an influential figure in shaping Indian foreign policy during his tenure.

Early Life and Education

  • Born in 1930, A. P. Venkateswaran hailed from a distinguished Tamil Brahmin family.
  • He pursued his education in India and abroad, excelling academically. Venkateswaran's sharp analytical mind and interest in global affairs eventually led him to join the Indian Foreign Service (IFS).

Career in Diplomacy

  • Venkateswaran joined the IFS in 1952, embarking on a distinguished career that spanned over three decades.
  • His postings across various countries allowed him to gain a deep understanding of international relations and diplomacy.
  • Over the years, he served in multiple capacities, including Indian ambassador to several countries, before ascending to the role of Foreign Secretary.
  • His tenure as Foreign Secretary was marked by clarity of vision and decisive action.
  • He played a key role in strengthening India's diplomatic relationships and addressing critical global issues.

Controversial Exit as Foreign Secretary

  • A. P. Venkateswaran's tenure as Foreign Secretary came to an abrupt and controversial end in January 1987.
  • During a press conference, then-Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi made an off-the-cuff remark suggesting that Venkateswaran would be stepping down, a statement that was perceived as unceremonious and unexpected.
  • In response, Venkateswaran resigned immediately, maintaining his dignity and composure.
  • This incident became one of the most talked-about events in Indian diplomatic history and highlighted Venkateswaran’s commitment to principles and self-respect.

Legacy and Contributions

  • A. P. Venkateswaran was admired for his deep understanding of global politics, impeccable professionalism, and straightforward approach.
  • He mentored many young diplomats, leaving a lasting impact on Indian diplomacy.
  • After retiring from the IFS, he continued to contribute to intellectual and policy discussions, sharing his insights on international affairs.

Personal Life

  • Venkateswaran was known for his wit, humility, and intellect.
  • Despite his high-ranking position, he maintained a down-to-earth demeanor and commanded immense respect among his colleagues and peers.

Passing

  • A. P. Venkateswaran passed away in 2014, leaving behind a legacy of excellence and integrity in Indian diplomacy.
  • His contributions to the nation and his principled stand during challenging times continue to inspire diplomats and civil servants in India.
