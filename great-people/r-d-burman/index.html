R. D. Burman

By Karthik Kumar D K | Published: Mar 3, 2024 11:57 AM IST
By Karthik Kumar D K , 3 March 2024

R. D. Burman, whose full name was Rahul Dev Burman, was a highly influential and celebrated music composer in the Indian film industry, particularly in Bollywood.

Here are some key points about R. D. Burman:

  • Birth: R. D. Burman was born on June 27, 1939, in Kolkata, British India.
  • Family Background: He was the only child of the renowned music composer Sachin Dev Burman and his lyricist wife Meera Dev Burman. Music was in his genes and he inherited a rich musical legacy.
  • Early Exposure to Music: R. D. Burman, known as Pancham to his close associates, started assisting his father in music composition from a young age. He played the harmonica in his father's compositions.
  • Debut as a Composer: R. D. Burman made his debut as an independent music composer with the film "Chhote Nawab" in 1961. However, his breakthrough came with the film "Teesri Manzil" (1966), directed by Vijay Anand.
  • Versatility: Pancham was known for his versatility and ability to experiment with various genres of music. He blended Indian and Western musical styles seamlessly.
  • Innovations: R. D. Burman introduced several innovations in Indian film music, including the use of electronic music, jazz and rock elements.
  • Collaborations: He had successful collaborations with lyricists like Majrooh Sultanpuri, Anand Bakshi and Gulzar and directors like Shakti Samanta, Nasir Hussain and Ramesh Sippy.
  • Hit Songs: R. D. Burman composed numerous hit songs that became iconic and are still popular today. Some of his notable compositions include "Yeh Jo Mohabbat Hai," "Dum Maro Dum," "Raina Beeti Jaye," and "Mehbooba Mehbooba."
  • Awards: He received several awards for his contributions to music, including Filmfare Awards.
  • Personal Life: R. D. Burman was married to Asha Bhosle, one of the most celebrated playback singers in India.
  • Death: Unfortunately, R. D. Burman's life was cut short and he passed away on January 4, 1994, at the age of 54.

R. D. Burman's influence on Indian film music is immeasurable and his compositions continue to be cherished by music enthusiasts. His ability to experiment and innovate while maintaining a strong connection with the Indian musical tradition earned him a special place in the history of Bollywood music.
