Saeed Akhtar Mirza

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Saeed Akhtar Mirza is an Indian filmmaker and screenwriter known for his work in Hindi cinema.

Here are some key points about Saeed Akhtar Mirza:

  • Early Life: Saeed Akhtar Mirza was born on June 30, 1943, in Mumbai, India.
  • Education: He studied at St. Xavier's College in Mumbai and later attended the Film and Television Institute of India (FTII) in Pune.
  • Filmmaking Career: Mirza is known for his socially relevant and politically conscious films that often address issues of marginalized communities and social injustice.
  • Collaboration with Kundan Shah: Saeed Akhtar Mirza is particularly known for his collaboration with filmmaker Kundan Shah. They worked together on the critically acclaimed television series "Nukkad" (1986) and the film "Jaane Bhi Do Yaaro" (1983), a satirical comedy.
  • Notable Films: Some of his notable films include "Albert Pinto Ko Gussa Kyon Aata Hai" (1980), "Mohandas" (2009) and "Salim Langde Pe Mat Ro" (1989).
  • Social and Political Themes: Mirza's films often explore themes related to the struggles of common people, the impact of political and economic systems and the complexities of human relationships.
  • Awards: His work has received critical acclaim and won several awards, including the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi for "Albert Pinto Ko Gussa Kyon Aata Hai."
  • Literary Works: Apart from filmmaking, Saeed Akhtar Mirza has written on social and political issues and has been associated with various social movements.
  • Social Activism: Mirza has been involved in social activism, advocating for the rights of marginalized communities and addressing issues of communal harmony.

Saeed Akhtar Mirza is known for his distinct voice in Indian cinema, using his films as a medium to highlight societal issues and human struggles. His contributions have left a significant impact on the world of cinema, particularly in the realm of socially conscious filmmaking.
