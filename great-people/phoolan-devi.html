Phoolan Devi

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Phoolan Devi, popularly known as the "Bandit Queen," was one of the most controversial and iconic figures in Indian history.

Here's an overview of her life:

  • Early Life: Phoolan Devi was born on August 10, 1963, into a low-caste Mallah (boatmen) family in the village of Ghura Ka Purwa, Uttar Pradesh, India. She faced discrimination and oppression from an early age due to her caste and gender.
  • Abduction and Abuse: At the age of 11, Phoolan Devi was forcibly married to a much older man and subjected to physical and sexual abuse. She eventually fled from her husband's home and returned to her village.
  • Criminal Activities: Phoolan Devi joined a gang of dacoits (bandits) led by Vikram Mallah, where she learned to wield a rifle and became involved in criminal activities, including robberies and kidnappings. She soon gained notoriety as the "Bandit Queen" and became one of the most wanted criminals in India.
  • Revenge and Retribution: Phoolan Devi's criminal activities were motivated by a desire for revenge against upper-caste men who had abused and oppressed her community. She targeted landlords and upper-caste individuals, often redistributing wealth to the poor and marginalized.
  • Arrest and Imprisonment: In 1983, Phoolan Devi surrendered to the authorities after negotiations with the government. She spent 11 years in prison, during which time she faced physical and sexual abuse.
  • Political Career: After her release from prison in 1994, Phoolan Devi entered politics and became a Member of Parliament (MP) from the Samajwadi Party. She focused on issues affecting marginalized communities, particularly women and lower castes.
  • Assassination: On July 25, 2001, Phoolan Devi was assassinated outside her official residence in New Delhi. The motive for her murder remains unclear, but it is believed to be linked to her past criminal activities and political career.
  • Legacy: Phoolan Devi's life continues to be a subject of fascination and controversy in India. She is celebrated as a symbol of resistance against caste and gender-based oppression, while also facing criticism for her criminal past and methods.

Phoolan Devi's life story has been the subject of numerous books, films and documentaries, including the acclaimed film "Bandit Queen" directed by Shekhar Kapur. She remains a complex and polarizing figure in Indian history, remembered for her defiance, resilience and struggle for justice.
