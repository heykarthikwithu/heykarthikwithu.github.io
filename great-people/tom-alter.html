Tom Alter

On30th Nov 2023, 2025-11-17T12:55:13+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Tom Alter (1950–2017) was an Indian film actor, television actor and stage performer of American descent. He was known for his versatility and the ability to portray a wide range of characters.

Here are some key points about Tom Alter:

  • Early Life: Tom Alter was born on June 22, 1950, in Mussoorie, Uttarakhand, India. His parents were American missionaries who settled in India.
  • Education: He studied at Woodstock School in Mussoorie and later attended Pune's Film and Television Institute of India (FTII).
  • Theater: Tom Alter began his career in the entertainment industry with English theater in India. He was associated with Prithvi Theatre in Mumbai.
  • Film Debut: He made his film debut in 1976 with the film "Charas."
  • Versatility: Tom Alter was known for his versatility and could effortlessly play roles of different nationalities. He played numerous character roles in Hindi cinema.
  • Television: Apart from films, Tom Alter was active in television. He appeared in various TV shows and series, including the popular "Zabaan Sambhalke."
  • Writer: Tom Alter was also a writer and authored several books, including fiction and sports-related works.
  • Sports Enthusiast: He was passionate about cricket and played the sport at the university level. He also worked as a sports journalist.
  • Awards: Tom Alter received the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award, in 2008, for his contributions to the field of arts and cinema.
  • Illness and Death: Tom Alter battled skin cancer in his later years. He passed away on September 29, 2017, in Mumbai.

Tom Alter's contributions to Indian cinema, television and literature were widely appreciated. He left a lasting impact on the industry and his ability to seamlessly adapt to various roles made him a beloved figure in Indian entertainment.
