Sahib Singh Verma

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Sahib Singh Verma was an Indian politician associated with the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).

Here are some key points about Sahib Singh Verma:

  • Birth: Sahib Singh Verma was born on May 15, 1943, in Delhi, India.
  • Political Career: Sahib Singh Verma had a long and influential political career. He was associated with the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) before joining active politics.
  • Trade Union Leader: Before entering mainstream politics, Sahib Singh Verma was known for his work as a trade union leader. He played a key role in the All India Trade Union Congress (AITUC).
  • BJP Leader: Sahib Singh Verma was a prominent leader of the BJP. He held various important positions within the party.
  • Chief Minister of Delhi: Sahib Singh Verma served as the Chief Minister of Delhi from 1996 to 1998. During his tenure, he worked on various developmental initiatives in the state.
  • Union Minister: He also served as the Union Minister of Labour in the Government of India.
  • Agricultural Reforms: Sahib Singh Verma was known for his focus on agricultural issues and was involved in introducing reforms related to agriculture.
  • Death: Sahib Singh Verma passed away on June 30, 2007, in a road accident near Jaipur, Rajasthan.

Sahib Singh Verma's political career spanned several decades and he played a significant role in both state and national politics. His contributions to trade union activities and agricultural reforms are notable aspects of his political legacy.
