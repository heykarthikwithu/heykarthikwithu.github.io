Berta Ruck (1878–1978) was a British author known for her popular romantic novels and fiction.

Here's an overview of her life and literary career:

  • Literary Career: Berta Ruck started her writing career in the early 20th century and quickly gained popularity for her romantic novels. Her novels often depicted the lives and romantic entanglements of young women and they were characterized by their engaging plots and vivid characters.
  • Popular Novels: Some of Berta Ruck's most well-known novels include "His Official Fiancee," "Love in a Mist," "The Greater Love," and "Green Trails and Upland Pastures." These novels were widely read and enjoyed by audiences, particularly in Britain and the United States.
  • Writing Style: Berta Ruck's writing style was characterized by its warmth, humor and charm. She had a talent for creating relatable characters and capturing the nuances of human relationships.
