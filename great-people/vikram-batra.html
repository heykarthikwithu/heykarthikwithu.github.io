Vikram Batra

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Captain Vikram Batra, fondly remembered as the “Sher Shah” of Kargil, is one of India’s most celebrated war heroes. His courage, patriotism, and selfless sacrifice during the Kargil War of 1999 turned him into a national icon, inspiring generations with his immortal words: “Yeh Dil Maange More!”

Early Life and Journey into the Army

Born on September 9, 1974, in Palampur, Himachal Pradesh, Vikram Batra grew up with a strong sense of discipline and love for the nation. From a young age, he was known for his fearless spirit and leadership qualities. After completing his education, he followed his passion by joining the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Dehradun, where he was commissioned into the 13th Battalion of the Jammu and Kashmir Rifles (13 JAK Rifles) in 1997.

The Lion of Kargil

During the Kargil War, Captain Batra displayed unmatched bravery in some of the most challenging operations carried out by the Indian Army. He played a pivotal role in recapturing key peaks from enemy forces, particularly Point 5140 and Point 4875, which were of immense strategic importance.

His fearless leadership in battle earned him the codename “Sher Shah” by the Indian Army and made him a legend among his comrades. With his infectious enthusiasm and famous call sign phrase “Yeh Dil Maange More!”, he symbolized the spirit of victory and determination.

The Ultimate Sacrifice

On July 7, 1999, Captain Batra made the supreme sacrifice while leading his troops in the operation to capture Point 4875. True to his character, he placed his men’s safety above his own and fought till his last breath. His bravery and devotion to duty earned him the Param Vir Chakra, India’s highest wartime gallantry award, posthumously.

Legacy of a National Hero

Even decades later, Vikram Batra’s story continues to ignite patriotic fervor across India. His life has been celebrated in books, documentaries, and films, including the acclaimed Bollywood movie Shershaah. Schools, institutions, and memorials across the country honor his name, ensuring that his sacrifice is never forgotten.

An Eternal Inspiration

Captain Vikram Batra’s life is not just about his heroism on the battlefield—it is a reminder of the spirit of selflessness, leadership, and love for the nation. His words and actions continue to inspire young Indians to rise above fear and serve with courage.

In essence, Vikram Batra was not just a soldier—he was a symbol of India’s indomitable spirit, a true son of the soil who lives forever in the nation’s heart.
