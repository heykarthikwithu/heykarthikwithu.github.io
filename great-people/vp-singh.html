V. P. Singh

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Vishwanath Pratap Singh, commonly known as V. P. Singh, was an Indian politician who served as the 7th Prime Minister of India.

Here are some key points about his life and political career:

  • Birth: V. P. Singh was born on June 25, 1931, in Allahabad, British India.
  • Education: He received his education at Allahabad University and later at the University of Pune.
  • Civil Service: V. P. Singh began his career in the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) in 1957. He held various administrative positions before entering politics.
  • Political Entry: He entered politics in the 1960s and joined the Indian National Congress party. He held several ministerial positions, including Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh.
  • Land Reforms: V. P. Singh is remembered for his efforts in implementing land reforms in Uttar Pradesh during his tenure as Chief Minister. These efforts aimed at improving the conditions of farmers and the rural poor.
  • Finance Minister: He served as the Finance Minister of India from 1984 to 1987 under Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi. His tenure witnessed the implementation of the Mandal Commission recommendations for reservations in government jobs for Other Backward Classes (OBCs).
  • Prime Ministership: V. P. Singh became the Prime Minister of India in 1989. His tenure is marked by various significant events, including the implementation of the Mandal Commission recommendations, which led to widespread protests and debates on social justice.
  • Economic Reforms: He initiated economic reforms, including a reduction in government expenditure and the introduction of economic liberalization measures.
  • Political Challenges: V. P. Singh's government faced challenges, including controversies related to the implementation of the Mandal Commission recommendations and internal political conflicts.
  • Resignation: V. P. Singh resigned as Prime Minister in 1990 after facing political challenges within his coalition government.
  • Janata Dal: He was associated with the Janata Dal party, which emerged from the Janata Party.
  • Later Years: V. P. Singh continued to be active in politics and served in various capacities. He also started his own political party, the Jan Morcha.
  • Death: V. P. Singh passed away on November 27, 2008, in New Delhi, India.

V. P. Singh's political career was marked by a commitment to social justice, economic reforms and efforts to address issues affecting marginalized communities. However, his tenure as Prime Minister was also marked by controversies and political challenges.
