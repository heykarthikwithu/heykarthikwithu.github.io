Meena Kumari

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Meena Kumari, born Mahjabeen Bano, was a legendary Indian film actress and poet.

Here are some key points about Meena Kumari:

  • Birth: Meena Kumari was born on August 1, 1932, in Bombay (now Mumbai), British India.
  • Early Career: Meena Kumari began her acting career as a child artist and later transitioned into lead roles. She made her debut as a heroine in the film "Baiju Bawra" (1952).
  • Versatility: Meena Kumari was known for her versatility as an actress. She excelled in portraying a wide range of characters, from tragic and dramatic roles to light-hearted and romantic ones.
  • Prolific Career: Over her career, Meena Kumari appeared in over 90 films in a career spanning three decades. Some of her notable films include "Pakeezah," "Sahib Bibi Aur Ghulam," "Baiju Bawra," and "Parineeta."
  • Pakeezah: "Pakeezah" (1972) is one of Meena Kumari's most iconic films. She not only acted in the lead role but also wrote and sang some of the songs in the film.
  • Awards: Meena Kumari received several awards for her performances, including several Filmfare Awards.
  • Personal Life: Meena Kumari's personal life was marked by tragedy, including a tumultuous marriage to filmmaker Kamal Amrohi. She faced health issues and her untimely death in 1972 at the age of 39 shocked the film industry and her fans.
  • Poetry: Apart from acting, Meena Kumari was a talented poet. Her poetry, often reflecting her emotions and experiences, was published posthumously in a collection titled "I write, I recite."
  • Legacy: Meena Kumari is remembered as the "Tragedy Queen" of Indian cinema. Her performances and the emotional depth she brought to her characters continue to be celebrated.
  • Homage: The Meena Kumari Memorial Award is given annually to a prominent woman in the field of literature, arts, or social service in her memory.

Meena Kumari's contribution to Indian cinema is significant and she remains an enduring and beloved figure in the history of Bollywood.
