Nisargadatta Maharaj

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Nisargadatta Maharaj, one of the most revered spiritual teachers of the 20th century, continues to inspire seekers worldwide with his profound teachings on self-realization and non-duality. Born on April 17, 1897, in Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharaj’s journey from a simple shopkeeper to a globally recognized sage is a story of deep devotion, inner awakening, and timeless wisdom.

From Householder to Realized Master

Nisargadatta lived an ordinary life for much of his early years. He ran a small shop, supported his family, and was immersed in the responsibilities of a householder. But destiny led him to his guru, Sri Siddharameshwar Maharaj, who initiated him into the teachings of Advaita (non-duality). Following his guru’s guidance with utmost sincerity, Nisargadatta experienced a profound transformation that revealed his true nature—the infinite self beyond body and mind.

The Teachings of "I Am"

At the heart of Nisargadatta Maharaj’s philosophy lies the simple yet deeply powerful teaching of dwelling in the sense of “I Am.” He urged seekers to hold onto this pure awareness, prior to identification with the body, mind, or worldly roles. His words were direct, uncompromising, and often stripped of ritualistic complexity.

His dialogues, later compiled in the spiritual classic “I Am That”, continue to guide truth-seekers across the globe. Through these teachings, he reminded humanity that liberation does not lie in external achievements but in recognizing our own eternal essence.

Legacy and Influence

Nisargadatta Maharaj never sought fame, yet his authenticity drew disciples and seekers from across the world. He spoke in his modest Mumbai apartment, often surrounded by people eager to hear his piercing wisdom. Despite his humble surroundings, his presence radiated profound clarity and fearlessness.

Today, Maharaj’s teachings are studied by spiritual aspirants worldwide. He remains one of the cornerstones of modern Advaita Vedanta, alongside teachers like Ramana Maharshi and J. Krishnamurti. His emphasis on self-inquiry and inner awareness continues to resonate with those seeking truth beyond dogma.

A Timeless Beacon of Wisdom

Nisargadatta Maharaj’s life reminds us that enlightenment is not reserved for monks or renunciates; it is available to anyone who earnestly seeks it. His message is simple yet eternal: Know yourself as the pure consciousness beyond all forms, and you are free.

In essence, Nisargadatta Maharaj was not just a teacher—he was a mirror reflecting the truth of existence itself.
