Suhasini Maniratnam Biography: Actress, Filmmaker & Philanthropist

By Karthik Kumar D K , 24 November 2025

Suhasini Maniratnam is a prominent Indian actress, filmmaker, and philanthropist, celebrated for her exceptional contributions to the South Indian film industry, particularly in Tamil and Telugu cinema.

Early Life and Background

Birth and Family:
Suhasini was born on August 15, 1961, in Paramakudi, Tamil Nadu, India, into a family closely associated with the film industry. Her father, Charuhasan, is a noted actor and lawyer, while her uncle is the legendary actor Kamal Haasan. Growing up in this creative environment influenced her passion for acting and cinema from a young age.

Acting Career

Debut and Breakthrough:
Suhasini made her acting debut in the Tamil film Nenjathai Killathe (1981), directed by her husband, acclaimed filmmaker Mani Ratnam. She quickly gained recognition for her nuanced performances.

Notable Films:
Some of her most acclaimed performances include:

  • Sindhu Bhairavi
  • Swati Mutyam
  • Anjali
  • Sathya

Apart from Tamil cinema, Suhasini has acted in Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Hindi films, establishing herself as a versatile and respected actress across South India.

Filmmaking and Screenwriting

Directorial Debut:
Suhasini ventured into filmmaking with the Tamil film Indira (1995), which she directed and starred in. The film, addressing social issues, received critical acclaim and showcased her versatility beyond acting.

Screenwriting:
Suhasini has also contributed as a screenwriter for Tamil films such as Sathya, Raja Paarvai, and Varushamellam Vasantham, demonstrating her storytelling skills and creative vision.

Awards and Recognition

Suhasini’s talent and dedication have earned her multiple accolades, including:

  • Several Filmfare Awards
  • Tamil Nadu State Film Awards

She is widely regarded as one of the finest actresses in the South Indian film industry.

Philanthropy and Social Work

Suhasini actively participates in philanthropic initiatives and social causes. She supports NGOs working for the welfare of women and children, education, and healthcare, making her a respected public figure beyond cinema.

Personal Life

Suhasini is married to acclaimed filmmaker Mani Ratnam, and they have a son, Nandan. The couple is considered one of Indian cinema’s most influential power couples, collaborating on several successful projects over the years.

Legacy and Impact

Suhasini Maniratnam’s contributions to Indian cinema as an actress, filmmaker, and screenwriter have left a lasting mark on the industry. Her versatility, creativity, and dedication to her craft continue to inspire aspiring actors and filmmakers alike.
