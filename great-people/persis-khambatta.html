Persis Khambatta

On25th Jun 2024, 2025-11-19T08:52:10+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Persis Khambatta (1948–1998) was an Indian actress and model who gained international fame for her roles in film and television.

Here's an overview of her life and career:

  • Early Life: Persis Khambatta was born on October 2, 1948, in Bombay, British India (now Mumbai, India). She was raised in a Parsi family and began her career as a model at a young age, appearing in various advertisements and fashion shows.
  • Miss India: In 1965, Persis Khambatta won the Femina Miss India pageant at the age of 17, becoming the first Indian woman to represent India at the Miss Universe pageant. Her striking looks and poise garnered international attention and she became a popular figure in the fashion industry.
  • Acting Career: Khambatta made her acting debut in the Indian film industry with the 1975 film "Shikhandi: The Adventures of a Gay Boy", directed by K. S. R. Das. She later gained international recognition for her role as Lieutenant Ilia, a bald Deltan navigator, in the 1979 science fiction film "Star Trek: The Motion Picture". Her portrayal of a bald alien character was a groundbreaking departure from traditional female roles in Hollywood.

  • Film and Television: Following her success in "Star Trek", Persis Khambatta appeared in several other films, including "Nighthawks" (1981) and "Megaforce" (1982). She also appeared in numerous television shows and miniseries, including "Magnum, P.I" and "Casablanca".
  • Activism: Outside of her acting career, Khambatta was involved in various social causes and charitable work. She was particularly passionate about advocating for the rights of children and worked with several organizations dedicated to child welfare.
  • Personal Life: Persis Khambatta was married twice, first to Hollywood actor Cliff Taylor in 1980 and later to hotelier Vikram Goldsmith. She did not have any children.
  • Passing: Sadly, Persis Khambatta passed away on August 18, 1998, at the age of 49, due to a heart attack in her Mumbai apartment. Her untimely death shocked and saddened fans around the world.

Persis Khambatta's talent, beauty and trailblazing role in "Star Trek" earned her a lasting place in the hearts of fans worldwide. She remains an iconic figure in both Indian and international cinema, remembered for her contributions to the entertainment industry.
