Amrish Puri

On29th Nov 2023, 2025-11-20T09:29:07+05:30 Listen Pause Resume Stop

Amrish Puri (1932–2005) was a legendary Indian actor, best known for his work in Hindi and other Indian language films. He was one of the most iconic villains in the history of Indian cinema and also portrayed several memorable character roles.

Here are some key points about Amrish Puri:

  • Early Life: Amrish Puri was born on June 22, 1932, in Lahore (now in Pakistan). His family moved to Mumbai after the partition of India.
  • Theater Career: Before entering films, Amrish Puri was actively involved in theater. He was associated with the famous Indian People's Theatre Association (IPTA).
  • Film Debut: Amrish Puri made his film debut in the Marathi film "Ghashiram Kotwal" in 1972. He later appeared in several Hindi films.
  • Breakthrough Role: His breakthrough role came with the film "Mashaal" (1984), where he played a negative character. This marked the beginning of his journey as a successful character actor and villain.
  • Iconic Villain: Amrish Puri became widely recognized for his portrayal of villains in Hindi cinema. His deep voice, intense eyes and powerful screen presence made him one of the most formidable antagonists in Bollywood.
  • Notable Films: Some of his notable films include "Mr. India," "Nagina," "Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge," "Gadar: Ek Prem Katha," and "Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom" (English film).
  • Versatility: While he was celebrated for his villainous roles, Amrish Puri displayed versatility by taking on various character roles, including positive and comedic characters.
  • Awards: Amrish Puri received several awards for his performances, including the Filmfare Award for Best Supporting Actor.
  • International Recognition: His role as Mola Ram in "Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom" brought him international recognition.
  • Personal Life: Amrish Puri was married to Urmila Divekar and they had two children, son Rajiv Puri and daughter Namrata Puri.
  • Death: Amrish Puri passed away on January 12, 2005, due to a cerebral hemorrhage. His death was a significant loss to the Indian film industry.

Amrish Puri's contributions to Indian cinema left an indelible mark and he remains one of the most celebrated actors in the history of Bollywood. His powerful performances and unforgettable dialogues continue to be remembered by fans and filmmakers alike.
