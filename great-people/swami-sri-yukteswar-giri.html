Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri (1855-1936) was an Indian yogi, spiritual teacher and author known for his profound spiritual wisdom and teachings. He was a disciple of the renowned yogi Paramahansa Yogananda and played a significant role in spreading the teachings of Kriya Yoga, a spiritual path focused on meditation, self-realization and union with the divine.

Here are some key aspects related to Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri and his contributions:

  1. Early Life and Spiritual Journey: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri was born as Priya Nath Karar on May 10, 1855, in Serampore, Bengal Presidency (now West Bengal), India. He displayed a deep inclination towards spirituality from an early age and sought guidance from various spiritual teachers. His search for spiritual truth eventually led him to become a disciple of Lahiri Mahasaya, an eminent yogi and proponent of Kriya Yoga.
  2. Teachings and Writings: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri is best known for his book "The Holy Science," published in 1894. In this book, he explained the principles of spiritual evolution, the harmony between science and spirituality and the cyclical nature of human consciousness. His writings emphasized the importance of aligning one's life with spiritual principles and seeking inner transformation through meditation and self-realization.
  3. Relationship with Paramahansa Yogananda: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri became the spiritual guru and mentor of Paramahansa Yogananda, the founder of Self-Realization Fellowship and author of the renowned book "Autobiography of a Yogi." Sri Yukteswar initiated Yogananda into the sacred path of Kriya Yoga and provided him with invaluable spiritual guidance.
  4. Propagation of Kriya Yoga: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri played a crucial role in spreading the teachings of Kriya Yoga, a powerful spiritual practice that combines meditation, breath control and self-discipline. He emphasized the importance of Kriya Yoga as a transformative path that enables seekers to attain spiritual enlightenment and union with the divine.
  5. Harmonizing Science and Spirituality: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri believed in the compatibility of science and spirituality. He emphasized that spirituality should not be divorced from scientific understanding and that both disciplines should work together to unlock the mysteries of the universe and human consciousness. His teachings aimed to bridge the gap between science and spirituality, promoting a holistic understanding of reality.
  6. Legacy and Influence: Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri's teachings and insights continue to inspire spiritual seekers around the world. His emphasis on the practice of Kriya Yoga, the integration of science and spirituality and the pursuit of self-realization have had a profound impact on the spiritual community. His wisdom and teachings have helped countless individuals on their spiritual journeys, fostering a deeper understanding of the divine and the nature of reality.

Swami Sri Yukteswar Giri's teachings and writings have left an indelible mark on the spiritual landscape, emphasizing the importance of meditation, self-realization and the harmonization of science and spirituality. His influence continues to guide seekers on the path of spiritual awakening and inner transformation.
