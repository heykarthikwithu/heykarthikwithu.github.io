A. K. Lohithadas

Listen Pause Resume Stop

A. K. Lohithadas (Ambazhathil Karunakaran Lohithadas) was a prominent Indian screenwriter, director and producer in the Malayalam film industry. He is best known for his contributions to Malayalam cinema, particularly for his work in scripting heart-touching and socially relevant films.

Here are some key points about A. K. Lohithadas:

  • Birth: A. K. Lohithadas was born on May 10, 1955, in Chalakudy, Thrissur, Kerala, India.
  • Early Career: He started his career in the film industry as a scriptwriter. His early works include collaboration with director Sathyan Anthikkad for films like "T. P. Balagopalan M.A." (1986) and "Nadodikkattu" (1987).
  • Scriptwriting: Lohithadas gained recognition for his ability to write scripts that addressed societal issues and portrayed realistic characters. His scripts often had a strong emotional appeal.
  • Notable Films: Some of the notable films written by A. K. Lohithadas include "Bharatham" (1991), "Kilukkam" (1991), "His Highness Abdullah" (1990), "Kireedam" (1989) and "Thaniyavarthanam" (1987).
  • Directorial Debut: Lohithadas made his directorial debut with the film "Bhoothakkannadi" (1997), which received critical acclaim.
  • Direction and Production: He went on to direct and produce several successful films. His directorial works include movies like "Kochu Kochu Santhoshangal" (2000), "Mazhavil Kavadi" (1989) and "Kasthooriman" (2003).
  • Awards: A. K. Lohithadas received several awards for his contributions to Malayalam cinema, including the Kerala State Film Award for Second Best Film for "Bhoothakkannadi" and "Kasthooriman."
  • Death: A. K. Lohithadas passed away on June 28, 2009, at the age of 54. His death was a loss to the Malayalam film industry.

A. K. Lohithadas is remembered as one of the most influential and respected figures in Malayalam cinema. His films continue to be celebrated for their strong storytelling, memorable characters and the ability to address relevant social issues.
