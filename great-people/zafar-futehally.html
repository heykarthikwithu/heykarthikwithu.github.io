Zafar Futehally

Zafar Rashid Futehally (1920–2013) was an Indian conservationist, writer and naturalist known for his contributions to wildlife conservation and environmental activism in India.

Here's an overview of his life and work:

  • Early Life and Education: Zafar Futehally was born on February 28, 1920, in Bangalore, India. He obtained his education from St. Joseph's College in Bangalore and later pursued higher studies at Bombay University.
  • Conservationist: Futehally was passionate about wildlife conservation from an early age. He played a key role in the establishment of several conservation organizations in India, including the Bombay Natural History Society (BNHS), where he served as the honorary secretary for many years. He was also involved in the conservation efforts of various national parks and sanctuaries across India.
  • Writer and Editor: Futehally was a prolific writer and editor, contributing articles and essays on wildlife, conservation and environmental issues to various newspapers, magazines and journals. He edited several publications, including the "Newsletter for Birdwatchers" and the "Hornbill" journal of the BNHS.
  • Books: Futehally authored and co-authored several books on birds, wildlife and conservation. Some of his notable works include "Common Birds," "A Pictorial Guide to the Birds of the Indian Subcontinent," and "The Book of Indian Birds," which he co-authored with Salim Ali.
  • Advocacy: Futehally was a vocal advocate for wildlife protection and environmental conservation. He used his writing and public speaking engagements to raise awareness about the importance of preserving India's rich biodiversity and natural heritage.
  • Recognition: Zafar Futehally received several awards and honors for his contributions to wildlife conservation, including the prestigious Padma Shri award from the Government of India in 2000.
  • Legacy: Zafar Futehally's legacy lives on through his writings, advocacy and conservation initiatives. He inspired generations of conservationists and nature enthusiasts in India and continues to be remembered as a pioneering figure in the field of wildlife conservation.

Zafar Futehally's tireless efforts to protect India's wildlife and natural habitats have left an indelible mark on the conservation movement in the country. His commitment to environmental stewardship and passion for nature continue to inspire conservationists around the world.
