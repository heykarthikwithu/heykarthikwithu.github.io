Kishore Kumar

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Kishore Kumar, born as Abhas Kumar Ganguly (August 4, 1929 – October 13, 1987), was an iconic Indian playback singer, actor, lyricist, composer, producer, director and screenwriter. He is one of the most celebrated and versatile artists in the history of Indian cinema.

Here are some key points about Kishore Kumar:

  • Early Life: Kishore Kumar was born in Khandwa, in present-day Madhya Pradesh, India, in a Bengali family. His elder brother, Ashok Kumar, was a famous actor.
  • Versatility: Kishore Kumar was known for his versatile singing style. He could sing in various genres, including romantic, sad and peppy songs. His ability to express a wide range of emotions through his voice contributed to his immense popularity.
  • Playback Singing: Kishore Kumar lent his voice to many leading actors in the Indian film industry, including Dev Anand, Rajesh Khanna, Amitabh Bachchan and many others. He sang numerous hit songs that continue to be popular.
  • Acting Career: Apart from singing, Kishore Kumar also had a successful career as an actor. Some of his notable films as an actor include "Chalti Ka Naam Gaadi", "Half Ticket" and "Padosan".
  • Personal Life: Kishore Kumar's personal life was marked by multiple marriages, including marriages to well-known personalities like Ruma Guha Thakurta and Madhubala.
  • Musical Compositions: Besides singing, Kishore Kumar composed music for several films. His composition for the film "Jhumroo" is particularly well-regarded.
  • Awards: Kishore Kumar received numerous awards for his contribution to Indian music, including several Filmfare Awards. He was posthumously awarded the "Lata Mangeshkar Award" by the Madhya Pradesh government.
  • Legacy: Kishore Kumar's legacy endures through his timeless songs. His voice is celebrated for its emotional depth and his songs are still widely played and admired.
  • Death: Kishore Kumar passed away on October 13, 1987, at the age of 58.

Kishore Kumar's impact on the Indian music industry is immeasurable and his songs continue to resonate with audiences across generations. He remains an enduring icon in the world of Indian cinema.
