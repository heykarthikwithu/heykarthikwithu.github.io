A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

Listen Pause Resume Stop

A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada (September 1, 1896 – November 14, 1977) was a revered Indian spiritual leader, scholar, and the founder of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), popularly known as the Hare Krishna Movement. Through his tireless efforts, Swami Prabhupada brought the teachings of Bhagavad Gita and Vaishnavism to the global stage, inspiring millions to embrace spirituality and a life centered on Krishna consciousness.

Early Life and Education

  • Birth Name: Abhay Charan De
  • Birth: September 1, 1896, in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India, into a devout Vaishnava family.
  • Parents: His father, Gour Mohan De, and mother, Rajani, were deeply religious and instilled in him the values of devotion to Lord Krishna.

Swami Prabhupada exhibited spiritual inclinations from a young age, participating in devotional practices and observing religious festivals dedicated to Lord Krishna.

  • Education: He attended Scottish Church College in Kolkata, where he studied English, philosophy, and economics. This education later helped him articulate and spread his teachings worldwide.
  • Marriage and Early Career: Prabhupada married Radharani Devi and worked as a manager for a pharmaceutical company. However, he remained deeply involved in spiritual activities and maintained his devotion to Krishna.

Meeting His Guru and Spiritual Calling

In 1922, Abhay Charan met his spiritual teacher, Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati Thakur, a prominent Vaishnava scholar and leader of the Gaudiya Math. This meeting was a turning point in his life, as Bhaktisiddhanta urged him to spread the teachings of Lord Krishna in the English-speaking world.

  • Initiation: In 1933, he was formally initiated into the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition.
  • Mission: Inspired by his guru's instructions, he began writing and translating spiritual texts and spreading the message of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, who emphasized the chanting of the Hare Krishna mantra as the means of spiritual liberation.

Founding of ISKCON and Global Mission

In 1959, at the age of 63, he renounced worldly life and took sannyasa (the renounced order of life), adopting the name A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami. Determined to fulfill his guru’s mission, he traveled to the United States in 1965.

Arrival in the United States

  • Swami Prabhupada arrived in New York City with minimal resources—only a trunk of books, a few personal belongings, and just $7 in cash.
  • Despite initial struggles, he began sharing the teachings of Lord Krishna through kirtans (devotional chanting) and lectures.

Establishment of ISKCON

  • In 1966, he officially founded the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) in New York City.
  • ISKCON soon grew into a global movement, with its core principles based on the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam.

Key Teachings of ISKCON

  1. Chanting the Hare Krishna Maha-Mantra:
    Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna Krishna, Hare Hare,
    Hare Rama, Hare Rama, Rama Rama, Hare Hare
    Chanting this mantra is believed to purify the mind and awaken devotion to Lord Krishna.

  2. Four Regulative Principles:

    • No meat-eating.
    • No intoxication.
    • No gambling.
    • No illicit sex.

  3. Bhakti Yoga: Devotion to Lord Krishna as the supreme path to spiritual realization.

Rapid Global Growth

  • Under Swami Prabhupada’s leadership, ISKCON expanded rapidly, establishing over 100 temples and spiritual centers worldwide.
  • He personally traveled extensively, delivering lectures, conducting ceremonies, and inspiring people of all backgrounds to embrace Krishna consciousness.

Literary Contributions

Swami Prabhupada’s literary work was monumental, as he sought to make the teachings of the Vedas and Bhagavad Gita accessible to a global audience.

Notable Translations and Works

  • Bhagavad Gita As It Is: His translation and commentary on the Bhagavad Gita became one of the most widely read and authoritative editions.
  • Srimad Bhagavatam: A comprehensive 18-volume translation and commentary on this foundational Vaishnava scripture.
  • Chaitanya Charitamrita: A biography and teachings of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, the founder of the Gaudiya Vaishnavism tradition.

Legacy in Literature

  • Swami Prabhupada’s works have been translated into over 80 languages, ensuring that his teachings reached a truly global audience.
  • His emphasis on presenting scriptures "as they are" helped preserve the authenticity of the Vedic tradition.

Philosophy and Spiritual Impact

Swami Prabhupada emphasized that the essence of life lies in serving and loving God, represented by Krishna in the Vaishnava tradition. His teachings focused on:

  • The unity of all beings as parts of Krishna.
  • The importance of a simple, God-centered life.
  • Spreading compassion and love through spiritual practices.

Awards and Recognition

Swami Prabhupada’s work was recognized both in India and globally:

  • Padma Bhushan (posthumously): For his contributions to literature and spirituality.
  • ISKCON’s festivals and temples have become major centers for cultural and spiritual activities worldwide.

Passing and Legacy

Swami Prabhupada passed away on November 14, 1977, in Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh, India, at the age of 81. His final moments were spent chanting the holy names of Krishna, surrounded by his disciples.

Legacy

  • ISKCON continues to flourish globally, with millions of followers and over 800 temples in countries such as the United States, Russia, and Australia.
  • His teachings have inspired people from all walks of life to embrace bhakti yoga and lead spiritually enriched lives.
  • The Bhaktivedanta Book Trust, established by Prabhupada, remains the world’s largest publisher of books on Krishna consciousness.

Summary

A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada was a spiritual visionary who revived and popularized the teachings of Vaishnavism on a global scale. Through his tireless dedication, ISKCON became a vibrant spiritual movement, offering humanity a path to inner peace and God-realization. His teachings, rooted in ancient wisdom yet relevant to modern life, continue to inspire millions, making him one of the most influential spiritual leaders of the 20th century.
