Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan

Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan was a distinguished Indian philosopher, statesman, and educationist who played a crucial role in shaping modern India’s intellectual and political landscape. He served as the first Vice President and the second President of India. A firm believer in the power of education and philosophy, he worked tirelessly to bridge Eastern and Western thought. His birthday, September 5, is celebrated as Teachers’ Day in India in recognition of his immense contributions to education.

V. O. Chidambaram Pillai

Valliappan Olaganathan Chidambaram Pillai, popularly known as V. O. Chidambaram Pillai, was a prominent Indian freedom fighter, lawyer, and entrepreneur. His relentless efforts to challenge British colonial rule, particularly in the maritime industry, made him a key figure in India's struggle for independence.

Hakam Sufi

Hakam Sufi was a distinguished Punjabi poet and lyricist known for his evocative and thought-provoking poetry. His literary contributions enriched Punjabi literature, and his work resonated deeply with readers due to its simplicity, profound themes, and cultural authenticity. His compositions often reflected themes of love, nature, spirituality, and the human experience, making him a cherished figure in the literary world.

Syed Mustafa Siraj

Syed Mustafa Siraj, a name synonymous with brilliance in Bengali literature, was a prolific writer, novelist, and short story writer whose works have left an indelible mark on the literary landscape of Bengal. Born on October 14, 1930, in Kolkata, India, Siraj’s literary journey spanned over five decades, during which he crafted stories that resonated deeply with readers across generations.

Dharamvir Bharati (1926–1997) was a celebrated Indian writer, poet, playwright, and journalist whose works have left an indelible mark on Hindi literature. Known for his profound insights into human emotions and the complexities of society, Bharati’s contributions spanned poetry, fiction, and essays, reflecting a deep understanding of India’s cultural and social ethos.

Ram Kishore Shukla

Ram Kishore Shukla (1918–2003) was a distinguished Indian politician, freedom fighter, and statesman who dedicated his life to the service of the nation and its people. Widely respected for his simplicity, humility, and integrity, Shukla played a pivotal role in the Indian freedom struggle and later contributed significantly to the development and welfare of the state of Madhya Pradesh as well as the country at large.

T. V. Sankaranarayanan

T. V. Sankaranarayanan (1945 – 2022) was a celebrated Carnatic vocalist, renowned for his rich voice, masterful improvisation, and faithful adherence to the traditional style of singing. As one of the most prominent Carnatic musicians of his time, he earned accolades for keeping the essence of the classical form alive while captivating audiences across the globe.

Bapu

Sattiraju Lakshmi Narayana, popularly known as Bapu (December 15, 1933 – August 31, 2014), was a legendary Indian cartoonist, painter, illustrator, and filmmaker. Renowned for his artistic brilliance, Bapu’s work transcended mediums, leaving an indelible mark on Indian cinema, art, and literature. His unique style of storytelling, complemented by his minimalist yet evocative illustrations, earned him critical acclaim and widespread admiration.
