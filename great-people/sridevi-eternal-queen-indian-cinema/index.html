Sridevi – The Eternal Queen of Indian Cinema

By Karthik Kumar D K | Published: Dec 12, 2025 3:32 PM IST
By Karthik Kumar D K , 12 December 2025

Sridevi, born as Shree Amma Yanger Ayyapan, is remembered as one of the greatest and most influential actresses in the history of Indian cinema. Her career, which began in childhood and extended across five major film industries, made her a household name and an evergreen icon. Even today, years after her passing, Sridevi continues to inspire millions with her talent, dedication, and unforgettable performances.

This article covers her early life, rise to fame, versatility, major films, achievements, and lasting legacy.

Early Life and Childhood Career

Sridevi was born on August 13, 1963, in Sivakasi, Tamil Nadu, into a Tamil-speaking family. She entered the world of cinema at a very young age. At just four years old, she made her acting debut as a child artist in the Tamil film “Kandhan Karunai” (1967).

By the time most children were learning to read, Sridevi had already appeared in Tamil, Telugu, and Malayalam films. Her natural expressions, innocence, and screen presence made filmmakers believe she was a rare and gifted child actor.

Some of her notable child roles included films like:

  • Thunaivan
  • Kumara Sambhavam
  • Badi Panthulu
  • Poompatta (which also won her the Kerala State Film Award for Best Child Artist)

Her early start laid the foundation for a long, successful journey in cinema.

Rise to Stardom in South Indian Cinema

As she grew older, Sridevi smoothly transitioned from child roles to lead roles. She acted in all major South Indian film industries, including Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada films.

During the late 1970s and early 1980s, Sridevi became a top heroine in Tamil and Telugu cinema. Her expressive eyes, natural acting style, and excellent dancing skills made her stand out from her contemporaries.

Some of her important South Indian films include:

  • Moondru Mudichu
  • 16 Vayathinile
  • Varumayin Niram Sivappu
  • Premabhishekam
  • Jagadeka Veerudu Athiloka Sundari
  • Kshana Kshanam

By this time, she was already recognized as a superstar in the South.

Entry Into Bollywood and National Fame

Sridevi entered Bollywood during a time when Hindi cinema was dominated by male actors. However, she soon changed that landscape. Her first major hit in Hindi was “Himmatwala” (1983). The film’s success, along with her song-and-dance popularity, established her as a leading actress in the Hindi film industry.

She followed it with several blockbusters, making her one of the only actresses capable of delivering huge hits on her own.

Some of her iconic Bollywood films include:

  • Sadma
  • Mr. India
  • Chandni
  • ChaalBaaz
  • Lamhe
  • Khuda Gawah
  • Judaai

Each film highlighted a different shade of her talent, proving her unmatched versatility.

Versatility and Acting Style

Sridevi was known for her ability to effortlessly shift between roles. She could make audiences laugh, cry, or fear for her character, all with equal ease.

What made Sridevi unique:

  1. Strong comic timing – As seen in ChaalBaaz and Mr. India.
  2. Emotional depth – Her role in Sadma remains one of the most emotional performances in Indian cinema.
  3. Graceful dancing – Songs like “Hawa Hawai” and “Mere Haathon Mein” remain evergreen.
  4. Powerful screen presence – Her expressions alone could communicate entire emotions.
  5. Ability to lead a film – She was among the first actresses who could carry a film without relying on a male superstar.

Her talent was often compared to top global performers, and many critics called her the first true female superstar of Indian cinema.

Breakthrough and Memorable Performances

1. Sadma (1983)

Sridevi delivered one of the greatest performances of her career in Sadma, where she played a woman with retrograde amnesia. The film is still regarded as a classic, and her role is considered one of the best performances in Indian film history.

2. Mr. India (1987)

As the iconic “Hawa Hawai,” Sridevi became an unstoppable sensation. Her comic scenes and glamorous presence stole the show.

3. ChaalBaaz (1989)

This film displayed her versatility in full strength. She played twin sisters with opposite personalities and delivered a career-defining performance.

4. Lamhe (1991)

Although not a commercial hit at the time, the film received critical acclaim. Today, it is considered a masterpiece, largely due to her powerful acting.

5. English Vinglish (2012)

This film marked Sridevi’s comeback after 15 years. Her portrayal of a housewife learning English received worldwide praise and reminded everyone why she was a legend.

Awards and Recognition

Sridevi received numerous awards throughout her long career, including:

  • Several Filmfare Awards across different languages
  • Padma Shri in 2013
  • Nandi Awards
  • Tamil Nadu State Film Awards
  • Kerala State Film Award
  • National Film Award (posthumously for "Mom")

She was widely loved outside India as well, especially in Gulf countries, where she was known as the “Empress of Indian Cinema.”

Return to Cinema and Later Years

After taking a break from acting to focus on her family, Sridevi returned with English Vinglish, which became both a critical and commercial success. She later appeared in Mom (2017), delivering another powerful performance.

Her ability to stay relevant across generations was rare. Even after decades in the industry, she maintained her charm and dedication.

Sudden Passing and Global Mourning

On February 24, 2018, Sridevi passed away unexpectedly at the age of 54. Her death shocked fans, the film industry, and people across the world. Tributes poured in from India, the Middle East, the United States, and many other countries.

Her funeral in Mumbai witnessed thousands of fans who came to bid goodbye to their favourite star.

Legacy of Sridevi

Sridevi’s contribution to Indian cinema is unmatched. She inspired countless actors and proved that a woman can lead a film and still make it a blockbuster.

Her legacy includes:

  • Over 50 years in cinema
  • More than 300 films
  • Performances across Tamil, Telugu, Hindi, Malayalam, and Kannada
  • Being India’s first true female superstar
  • Setting new standards for acting and dance
  • Opening doors for future actresses to lead films

Sridevi remains an eternal symbol of grace, talent, and dedication. Her films continue to be watched, celebrated, and loved by millions.

Conclusion

Sridevi was not just an actress. She was an emotion for millions of fans. Her journey from a child artist to a global cinema icon is a story of hard work, passion, and pure talent. Even today, she lives on through her films, songs, and the memories she left behind.
