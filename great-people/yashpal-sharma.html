Yashpal Sharma

Yashpal Sharma (1954–2021) was a former Indian cricketer who played a key role in the Indian cricket team during the 1983 Cricket World Cup, where India emerged as the surprise winners.

Here's an overview of his life and cricketing career:

  • Early Life: Yashpal Sharma was born on August 11, 1954, in Ludhiana, Punjab, India.
  • Domestic Career: Yashpal Sharma made his debut in first-class cricket in 1973-74 playing for Punjab. He was known for his solid batting technique and ability to score runs consistently.
  • International Career: Yashpal Sharma made his debut for the Indian cricket team in a Test match against England in 1978. He played 37 Test matches for India, scoring 1606 runs at an average of 33.45, including two centuries and nine half-centuries. He was known for his gritty batting performances, particularly against pace bowling.
  • 1983 Cricket World Cup: Yashpal Sharma played a crucial role in India's historic victory in the 1983 Cricket World Cup. He was one of the top run-scorers for India in the tournament, scoring 240 runs in eight matches at an average of 34.28. His contributions with the bat, including vital knocks in crucial matches, were instrumental in India's success.

  • Notable Performances: Yashpal Sharma's memorable innings in the 1983 World Cup included a crucial 89 against the West Indies in the group stage and a match-winning 61 against England in the semifinal.
  • Post-Retirement: After retiring from international cricket, Yashpal Sharma remained involved in the sport as a coach, commentator and selector. He served as a national selector for the Indian cricket team.
  • Recognition: Yashpal Sharma's contributions to Indian cricket, particularly during the 1983 World Cup, earned him widespread recognition and respect among cricket enthusiasts in India.
  • Passing: Yashpal Sharma passed away on July 13, 2021, due to a cardiac arrest, leaving behind a legacy as one of India's cricketing heroes.

Yashpal Sharma will be remembered as one of the stalwarts of Indian cricket, especially for his memorable performances during the 1983 Cricket World Cup, which remains one of the most significant milestones in Indian cricket history.
