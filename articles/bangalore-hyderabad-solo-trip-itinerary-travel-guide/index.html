Bangalore to Hyderabad: Solo Trip Itinerary & Travel Guide

Bangalore to Hyderabad: Solo Trip Itinerary & Travel Guide

Planning a trip from Bangalore to Hyderabad can be an exciting adventure! One day trip or Two day trip from Bangalore to Hyderabad, If you are Solo riding on a MotorBike or You are a Family driving in a Car, then this two to three days plan works very well to cover places in Hyderabad.

Route

Bangalore → Anantapur → Kurnool → Hyderabad → Bangalore

Here's a suggested itinerary to make the most of your journey:

Bangalore to Hyderabad

  • Start your journey early in the morning from Bangalore. The distance between Bangalore and Hyderabad is approximately 550 kilometers, which can take around 7-8 hours by road.
  • Take the Bangalore-Hyderabad highway (NH44) and enjoy the scenic drive through the countryside.
  • Along the way, you can take short breaks at places like Anantapur or Kurnool to stretch your legs and grab a quick bite.
  • Reach Hyderabad by late afternoon or early evening.
  • Check into your accommodation and rest for a while.
  • In the evening, explore the local markets like Laad Bazaar and Charminar area for some shopping and street food experiences.
  • Spend the night in Hyderabad.

Exploring Hyderabad

  • Start your day with a visit to the iconic Charminar, a historical monument and a symbol of Hyderabad.
  • Explore the nearby Mecca Masjid, one of the oldest and largest mosques in India.
  • Head to the nearby Chowmahalla Palace, a stunning palace complex that was once the seat of the Nizams of Hyderabad.
  • Afterward, visit the Salar Jung Museum, which houses an extensive collection of art, artifacts and antiques from around the world.
  • In the afternoon, indulge in a delicious Hyderabadi biryani for lunch at one of the famous local restaurants.
  • After lunch, visit the Golconda Fort, known for its architectural brilliance and panoramic views of the city.
  • If time permits, you can also explore the Qutub Shahi Tombs nearby.
  • In the evening, take a leisurely stroll around the Hussain Sagar Lake and enjoy the beautiful Necklace Road.
  • You can end your day by trying out some Hyderabadi street food or visiting a popular restaurant for a traditional meal.
  • Spend another night in Hyderabad.

Hyderabad Sightseeing and Return to Bangalore

  • Start your day by visiting the Birla Mandir, a serene and beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Venkateshwara.
  • Next, explore the Nehru Zoological Park, which is home to a wide variety of animals and birds.
  • If you're interested in technology, visit the Birla Science Museum and Planetarium.
  • In the afternoon, you can go shopping at popular malls like Inorbit Mall, Forum Sujana Mall, or GVK One Mall, where you can find a mix of international and Indian brands.
  • Alternatively, you can visit the Ramoji Film City, located on the outskirts of Hyderabad, which is one of the largest film studios in the world.
  • Afterward, start your journey back to Bangalore. The return trip may take a similar amount of time as the onward journey.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive and reach Bangalore by late evening.
  • End your trip with some good memories of your solo adventure.

Remember to check the current travel restrictions, road conditions and weather forecasts before embarking on your trip. It's always a good idea to have a backup plan and be flexible with your itinerary. Enjoy your solo trip from Bangalore to Hyderabad.
