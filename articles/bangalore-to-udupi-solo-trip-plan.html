Bangalore to Udupi Solo Trip Plan: Temple, Beaches & Tips

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Solo trip from Bangalore to Udupi can be a delightful experience, as you'll get to explore the pristine beaches, ancient temples and delicious cuisine of this coastal town in Karnataka.

Udupi is one of the top tourist attractions in Karnataka and has various educational institutions. It is notable for the Krishna Temple and is also known as the temple city.[3] It also lends its name to the popular Udupi cuisine, is also known as Parashurama Kshetra and is famous for Kanakana kindi

Here's a suggested itinerary for your trip:

Route

Bangalore -> Hassan -> Sakleshpura -> Mangaluru -> Udupi -> Mangaluru -> Sakleshpura -> Bangalore

Bangalore to Udupi

  • Start your trip early in the morning from Bangalore.
  • The distance between Bangalore and Udupi is approximately 405 kilometers and it takes around 7-8 hours by road.
  • Take the route via Mangalore to reach Udupi.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive through the countryside and coastal regions of Karnataka.
  • Reach Udupi by late afternoon or early evening.
  • Check into your accommodation and take some time to relax and freshen up.
  • In the evening, you can visit the famous Udupi Sri Krishna Temple and witness the evening rituals.
  • Explore the Car Street, known for its vibrant shops and eateries.
  • Spend the night in Udupi.

Beaches and Cuisine

  • Start your day by visiting the beautiful beaches in Udupi.
  • Head to Malpe Beach, known for its golden sands and tranquil ambiance.
  • Take a boat ride to St. Mary's Islands, a group of pristine islands with unique rock formations.
  • Enjoy leisurely walks along the beach and soak in the coastal beauty.
  • In the afternoon, you can visit the Manipal End Point, a scenic viewpoint offering panoramic views of the surroundings.
  • Explore the local markets and indulge in some shopping for traditional items or Udupi cuisine ingredients.
  • Don't miss out on tasting the famous Udupi cuisine, especially the delicious vegetarian dishes like Masala Dosa and Idli.
  • In the evening, you can relax at the beach or visit the nearby Kapu Lighthouse for a beautiful sunset view.
  • Spend another night in Udupi.

Temples and Departure

  • Start your day by visiting the other famous temples in Udupi.
  • Explore the Anegudde Vinayaka Temple, dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located near Udupi.
  • Visit the Chandramouleshwara Temple, a centuries-old Shiva temple known for its intricate carvings.
  • If time permits, you can also visit the Pajaka Kshetra, the birthplace of the great saint Madhvacharya.
  • In the afternoon, start your journey back to Bangalore.
  • Reach Bangalore by late evening and conclude your solo trip to Udupi with beautiful memories.

Please note that this itinerary provides a general outline for your trip and you can modify it according to your preferences and the time you have available. Also check the article image for different route which is via Karkala & Belur, you can try route while going or coming back from Udupi. Make sure to check the current travel guidelines, road conditions and weather forecasts before embarking on your journey. Enjoy your solo adventure in Udupi.
