Best food to have for better bowel movement

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Maintaining good bowel movements is essential for overall digestive health.

Here are some foods that can promote healthy bowel movements:

  • Fiber-Rich Foods:
    • Whole Grains: Brown rice, quinoa, oats and whole wheat.
    • Legumes: Beans, lentils and chickpeas.
    • Fruits: Berries, apples, pears, prunes and kiwi.
    • Vegetables: Broccoli, carrots, sweet potatoes and leafy greens.
  • Prunes (Dried Plums): Prunes are known for their natural laxative effect due to their high fiber content and a natural sugar alcohol called sorbitol.
  • Figs: Figs are high in fiber and can promote regular bowel movements.
  • Psyllium Husk: Psyllium is a soluble fiber that can help soften stools and promote regular bowel movements. It's commonly found in products like Metamucil.
  • Yogurt with Probiotics: Yogurt containing probiotics (live beneficial bacteria) can help regulate gut function and promote a healthy digestive system.
  • Hydration: Drinking plenty of water is crucial for maintaining soft and bulky stools. Dehydration can lead to constipation.
  • Flaxseeds: Flaxseeds are rich in fiber and can be added to yogurt, smoothies, or cereals.
  • Chia Seeds: Chia seeds absorb water and form a gel-like consistency, which can help soften stools and promote regular bowel movements.
  • Leafy Greens: Vegetables like spinach, kale and Swiss chard are rich in fiber and can contribute to regular bowel movements.
  • Ginger: Ginger has been traditionally used to alleviate digestive discomfort and promote healthy digestion.
  • Papaya: Papaya contains an enzyme called papain, which aids in digestion. It's also rich in fiber.
  • Olive Oil: Consuming a small amount of olive oil may help lubricate the digestive system and promote bowel regularity.
  • Aloe Vera Juice: Aloe vera juice may have a mild laxative effect and can help soothe the digestive tract.

Remember, it's essential to maintain a balanced diet and not rely solely on specific foods. Additionally, if you experience persistent changes in bowel habits or have concerns about digestion, it's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional for a thorough evaluation and personalized advice.
