Abhaya Kshethram: A Sanctuary of Hope & Dignity

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Nestled near Renigunta Road in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh, Abhaya Kshethram is a charitable home dedicated to providing care, dignity, and hope to those society often overlooks: physically and mentally challenged orphans, destitute women, and the elderly. (Abhaya Kshethram)

Founding & Vision

Founded in 2007, Abhaya Kshethram was launched on the occasion of Mother Teresa’s birthday, symbolizing its deep commitment to selfless service.

It operates as a registered charitable trust under the Indian Trusts Act and holds 12A exemption under the Income Tax Act, ensuring transparency and accountability in all its social work.

The institution’s guiding philosophy is simple yet profound:

“To serve God by serving humanity.”

Who Resides at Abhaya Kshethram

The founder started with 2 residents in 2007, Abhaya Kshethram houses over ~200 residents, including:

  • Physically challenged orphans
  • Mentally challenged orphans
  • Abandoned or destitute women
  • Elderly persons without support

Each resident is considered a “student” at Abhaya Kshethram — irrespective of age or condition — reflecting the idea that learning, inclusion, and dignity are lifelong.

Leadership: Dr. S. A. Thasleem Sultana

At the heart of Abhaya Kshethram’s journey is Dr. S. A. Thasleem Sultana, its founder and director.

A scholar and social reformer, she holds a PhD in Special Education from Sri Venkateswara University, Tirupati, with her research focusing on inclusive education for children with special needs.

Her dedication has earned national recognition, including:

  • Rajiv Gandhi Manav Seva Award (2010) — Ministry of Women & Child Development
  • 100 Inspiring Women Award (2016) — at Rashtrapati Bhavan
  • Devi Award (2016) — presented by the Vice President of India

Under her compassionate leadership, Abhaya Kshethram continues to grow as a space of healing, empowerment, and unconditional love.

Daily Life & Operations

  • The residents are accommodated across three buildings, organized by age / need.
  • Their daily needs (food, clothing, medication, care) are met through donations from individuals, small firms, and through organizations such as Hope for Humanity.
  • Notably, Abhaya Kshethram does not depend on government funding.
  • The institution also emphasizes meaningful engagement: residents contribute via crafts (making bags, greeting cards, stitching) in line with their abilities.

Impact & Community Recognition

  • Abhaya Kshethram supports around ~200 residents, including 35 physically challenged orphans, 67 mentally challenged orphans, 48 destitute women, and 50 elderly individuals.
  • Over the years, the institution has received steadfast support from countless well-wishers, volunteers, and charitable contributors — each extending help in their own way, whether through donations, supplies, visits, or time spent with the residents.
  • This collective spirit of compassion has allowed Abhaya Kshethram to sustain its services and continue providing a life of dignity to those who once had none.
  • It is rated ~4.4 stars by local community users. (Justdial)

Challenges & Aspirations

Running a large charitable home without government funding poses constant challenges — from raising funds for medicine to maintaining buildings and utilities.

Still, Abhaya Kshethram continues to move forward with faith and purpose.

Future goals include:

  • Expanding capacity to serve more people in need
  • Enhancing medical and therapeutic facilities
  • Building sustainable livelihood programs for residents

Conclusion

Abhaya Kshethram is more than a home — it is a testament to what compassion anchored in action can achieve. Under Dr. Sultana’s stewardship, it stands as a living beacon, reminding us that dignity, respect, and hope must not be denied to any soul, regardless of ability or circumstance.
