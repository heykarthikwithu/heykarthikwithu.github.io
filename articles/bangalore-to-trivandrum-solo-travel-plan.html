Bangalore to Trivandrum: Solo Trip Itinerary & Travel Tips

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Traveling solo from Bangalore to Trivandrum is a refreshing experience, especially if you enjoy culture, beaches, history and relaxed city life. Trivandrum (Thiruvananthapuram), the capital of Kerala, blends tradition with natural beauty, making it a perfect destination for a peaceful solo getaway.

Here is a detailed and easy-to-follow plan for your trip.

How to Travel From Bangalore to Trivandrum

By Air

  • Fastest option
  • Direct flights available
  • Takes around 1 hour 15 minutes

By Train

  • Many trains operate daily
  • Comfortable and budget-friendly

By Road

  • Around 695 km from Bangalore
  • Takes 12–14 hours
  • Scenic route via NH44

Choose based on your time, budget and comfort.

Day 1: Arrival in Trivandrum

Arrive in Trivandrum by flight, train or bus, depending on your preference.

  • Check into your accommodation and take some time to rest.
  • Start exploring the city’s famous landmarks.

Padmanabhaswamy Temple
One of the richest and most visited temples in India. Its architecture and historical significance make it a must-visit place.

Napier Museum
A great spot to learn about Kerala’s culture, art and heritage.

Kuthiramalika Palace
Famous for its wooden carvings and royal collections.

Food for the Day
Enjoy a traditional Kerala meal such as:

  • Appam with stew
  • Puttu and kadala curry
  • Kerala thali

This day gives you a beautiful introduction to Trivandrum’s culture.

Day 2: Beach Day at Kovalam or Varkala

Spend this day connecting with the sea, relaxing on the sand and enjoying coastal Kerala.

Kovalam Beach
Known for crescent-shaped shores, calm waters and lighthouse views.

Varkala Beach
Famous for its cliffside walkway, peaceful atmosphere and clean golden sand.

Things You Can Do

  • Water sports like parasailing or surfing
  • Sunbathing and relaxing
  • Swimming (where allowed)
  • Eating fresh seafood at beachside cafes
  • Watching a stunning sunset

End the day with a peaceful walk along the shoreline.

Day 3: Backwaters, Greenery and Local Life

No Kerala trip is complete without experiencing the famous backwaters.

Poovar Backwaters
A beautiful location where the river meets the sea. The boat ride here passes through dense greenery and mangroves.

Veli Tourist Village
Ideal for boating, walking trails and lakeside views.

Poovar Island
A peaceful retreat surrounded by water on all sides.

Activities on Day 3

  • Backwater boat cruise
  • Bird watching
  • Visiting small villages
  • Exploring local markets in the evening

Buy spices, banana chips, handicrafts and small souvenirs from Trivandrum markets before you return.

Day 4: Departure

Depending on your schedule:

  • Visit any remaining attractions
  • Relax at your hotel
  • Take a final walk around the city

Proceed to Trivandrum International Airport or Trivandrum Central Railway Station for your return journey to Bangalore.

Why Trivandrum Is Perfect for Solo Travelers

1. Rich Cultural Experience
The city is known for its temples, museums, palaces and historic streets that give you a deep look into Kerala’s traditions.

2. Natural Beauty Everywhere
Trivandrum offers:

  • Beaches
  • Backwaters
  • Green landscapes
  • Calm surroundings

It’s a great destination for peaceful travel.

3. Amazing Kerala Food
You can enjoy:

  • Seafood
  • Kerala-style biryani
  • Appam
  • Stew
  • Tapioca dishes

Solo food lovers will enjoy exploring local restaurants.

4. Safe and Tourist-Friendly
Trivandrum is known for:

  • Good transport
  • Clean localities
  • Helpful people
  • Many stay options

This makes it ideal for solo travelers.

Best Time To Visit Trivandrum

October to February
Pleasant climate and ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

During Festivals
Visiting during festivals like:

  • Onam
  • Vishu
  • Attukal Pongala

… lets you experience Kerala’s culture more deeply.

Final Tips for Solo Travelers

  • Carry basic medicines and essential documents
  • Wear comfortable clothes and footwear
  • Use local transport like autos, buses and cabs
  • Respect temple dress codes
  • Stay hydrated during daytime travel

Enjoy a peaceful and memorable solo trip to Trivandrum.
