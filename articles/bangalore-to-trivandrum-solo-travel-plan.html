Bangalore to Trivandrum Solo Travel Plan

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Traveling solo from Bangalore to Trivandrum offers a wonderful opportunity to explore the cultural richness, natural beauty and vibrant atmosphere of Kerala's capital city.

Here's a solo travel plan for your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Trivandrum
    • Arrive in Trivandrum by flight, train, or bus.
    • Check into your accommodation and freshen up.
    • Explore local attractions like Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Napier Museum and Kuthiramalika Palace.
    • Enjoy a traditional Kerala meal at a local restaurant.
  • Day 2: Beach Day
    • Spend the day relaxing at one of Trivandrum's beautiful beaches, such as Kovalam or Varkala.
    • Indulge in water sports activities, sunbathe on the golden sands and witness a mesmerizing sunset by the sea.
    • Enjoy fresh seafood at a beachside restaurant.

  • Day 3: Backwaters and Nature
    • Take a day trip to explore the serene backwaters of Poovar or Veli.
    • Enjoy a boat ride through the backwaters, surrounded by lush greenery and coconut palms.
    • Visit nearby attractions like Poovar Island or Veli Tourist Village.
    • Return to Trivandrum in the evening and explore local markets for souvenirs.
  • Day 4: Departure
    • Depending on your departure time, you can explore any remaining attractions or relax at your accommodation.
    • Check out and proceed to Trivandrum International Airport or Trivandrum Central Railway Station for your onward journey.

Why Travel to Trivandrum Solo:

  • Cultural Exploration: Trivandrum is known for its rich cultural heritage, with attractions like the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, Napier Museum and Kuthiramalika Palace offering insights into Kerala's history and traditions.

Google Ad 2

  • Natural Beauty: Trivandrum is surrounded by scenic landscapes, including beaches like Kovalam and Varkala, backwaters and lush greenery, providing opportunities for relaxation and rejuvenation.
  • Culinary Delights: Experience Kerala's delicious cuisine, including traditional dishes like appam with stew, seafood delicacies and authentic Kerala-style biryani.
  • Solo-Friendly Atmosphere: Trivandrum is a tourist-friendly city with good connectivity, safety and plenty of accommodation options suitable for solo travelers.

How to Travel:

  • By Air: The fastest way to reach Trivandrum from Bangalore is by flight. Trivandrum International Airport is well-connected to major cities across India.
  • By Train: Trivandrum Central Railway Station is a major railway hub in Kerala, with several trains running between Bangalore and Trivandrum on a daily basis.
  • By Road: You can also travel by road from Bangalore to Trivandrum via NH44. The distance is approximately 695 kilometers and the journey takes around 12-14 hours by bus or car.

Google Ad 3

When to Travel:

  • Weather: Trivandrum experiences a tropical climate, with hot summers and monsoon rains. The best time to visit is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for exploring the city and nearby attractions.
  • Festivals: Consider timing your visit during festivals like Onam or Vishu to experience Kerala's vibrant culture, traditional rituals and colorful celebrations.

Ensure you have travel insurance, carry necessary documents and follow safety guidelines during your solo trip to Trivandrum. Enjoy your adventure in the capital city of Kerala.
