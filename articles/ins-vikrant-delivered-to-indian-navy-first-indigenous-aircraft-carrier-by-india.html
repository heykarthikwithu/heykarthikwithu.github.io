INS Vikrant Delivered to Indian Navy: Indias First Indigenous Aircraft Carrier

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

In a historic milestone for India’s defense and shipbuilding industry, INS Vikrant, also known as Indigenous Aircraft Carrier 1 (IAC-1), has been delivered to the Indian Navy. This achievement marks India’s entry into the exclusive league of countries capable of designing and building aircraft carriers domestically.

INS Vikrant is not just a warship; it is a symbol of technological excellence, national pride, and Atma Nirbhar Bharat (self-reliant India). This article explores the history, features, and significance of this majestic carrier.

What is INS Vikrant?

INS Vikrant is the first aircraft carrier built entirely in India. Constructed by the Cochin Shipyard Limited (CSL), the carrier represents decades of research, design, and engineering.

The name Vikrant means courageous in Sanskrit, reflecting the strength and resilience of the Indian Navy. The ship’s motto, “Jayema Saṁ Yudhi Sprdhah”, translates to “I defeat those who fight against me”, symbolizing its mission to protect India’s maritime interests.

Timeline and Development of INS Vikrant

The journey of INS Vikrant spans over two decades, showcasing India’s persistence and technological capabilities. Here is a brief overview:

  • 1999: The design work of the aircraft carrier began, setting the foundation for a fully indigenous project.
  • 2009: The keel of the ship was laid, formally marking the start of construction.
  • 2011: The carrier was floated out of its dry dock, an important milestone in shipbuilding.
  • 2013: INS Vikrant was officially launched, moving closer to operational readiness.
  • 2020: Basin trials were completed, testing the ship’s systems in a controlled environment.
  • 2021: The carrier began sea trials to evaluate its performance in real maritime conditions.

After years of dedicated work, the ship has now been delivered to the Indian Navy, ready to strengthen India’s naval capabilities.

Significance of INS Vikrant

1. Boost to Make in India Initiative

INS Vikrant is a prime example of India’s Make in India initiative, emphasizing domestic production and technological self-reliance. With 76% of its content being indigenous, the carrier highlights India’s growing expertise in defense manufacturing.

2. Joining an Elite Group of Countries

With the delivery of INS Vikrant, India has joined a small group of nations capable of designing and constructing aircraft carriers domestically, a feat that requires advanced engineering and naval expertise.

3. Economic and Strategic Impact

  • The project was completed in three phases at a total cost of just under Rs 20,000 crore.
  • It demonstrates India’s ability to execute large-scale defense projects efficiently.
  • The carrier enhances India’s strategic maritime presence and strengthens naval defense along crucial sea routes.

Features of INS Vikrant

INS Vikrant is not just large and powerful; it is also technologically advanced. Some of its key features include:

  1. Support for Various Aircraft
    1. Designed to carry a 30-aircraft air wing, including:
      1. MIG-29K fighter planes
      2. Light Combat Aircraft (LCA)
      3. Advanced Light Helicopters
      4. Kamov-31 helicopters
      5. MH-60R multi-role helicopters
  2. High Level of Automation
    1. The ship incorporates automated systems for navigation, machinery operation, and survivability, reducing the reliance on manual operation and enhancing efficiency.
  3. Enhanced Survivability
    1. Advanced systems ensure the carrier can withstand attacks, protect its crew, and maintain operational capabilities even under challenging conditions.
  4. Modern Design and Construction
    1. Designed using the latest naval architecture techniques
    2. Equipped with state-of-the-art sensors, communication systems, and weaponry

Aircraft and Flight Trials

INS Vikrant is designed to operate a diverse range of aircraft. Flight trials are a crucial part of the ship’s operational readiness.

  • The aircraft complement includes fighter jets, helicopters, and light combat aircraft.
  • Flight trials are expected to be completed by mid-2023, after which the ship will be fully operational.
  • These trials will test take-offs, landings, and coordination of aircraft, ensuring the carrier functions seamlessly in combat and defense scenarios.

Strategic Importance of INS Vikrant

1. Enhancing Maritime Security

INS Vikrant significantly boosts India’s naval strength, providing:

  • Air cover over sea operations
  • Capability to project power in the Indian Ocean Region (IOR)
  • Support for humanitarian and disaster relief missions

2. Strengthening Defense Manufacturing

The successful construction of INS Vikrant demonstrates India’s growing capabilities in indigenous defense production, reducing dependency on foreign imports. This aligns with India’s vision of Atma Nirbhar Bharat (self-reliant India).

3. Technological Milestone

From design to commissioning, INS Vikrant represents a technological leap for Indian shipbuilding:

  • Advanced hull design and construction techniques
  • State-of-the-art automation and safety features
  • Ability to integrate and operate modern aircraft systems

Future of INS Vikrant in Indian Navy

INS Vikrant is expected to play a pivotal role in India’s maritime strategy:

  • Fully operational by 2023: Once flight trials are completed, it will become a key component of India’s aircraft carrier fleet.
  • Multi-role capabilities: From combat missions to disaster relief, the carrier offers versatile support.
  • Training and development: It will serve as a platform for naval aviation training and development of future aircraft carrier technologies.

Conclusion

The delivery of INS Vikrant to the Indian Navy is a historic achievement for India. As the first indigenous aircraft carrier, it represents technological excellence, national pride, and the spirit of self-reliance.

With its advanced features, support for multiple aircraft, and strategic importance, INS Vikrant will enhance India’s maritime security and strengthen its position among naval powers worldwide.

This milestone is not just a victory for the Indian Navy but also a celebration of India’s engineering, innovation, and defense capabilities.

INS Vikrant truly embodies the courage and resilience reflected in its name and motto: “Jayema Saṁ Yudhi Sprdhah” – I defeat those who fight against me.
