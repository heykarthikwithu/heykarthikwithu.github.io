Dealing with Uncertainty, To Be Happier and Less Anxious

By Karthik Kumar D K , 17 November 2025

Uncertainty is a part of life. It shows up in health, finances, relationships, career, family situations, and sometimes in unexpected global events like the Covid pandemic. As human beings, everyone naturally wants to feel safe and in control. So when life becomes unpredictable, the mind reacts with stress, anxiety, fear, and a constant feeling of “What if?”

Some people enjoy taking risks, but everyone has a limit. When uncertainty becomes too much to handle, it can make life feel heavy and directionless. But it is important to remember one thing: you are not alone. Many people around you are facing the same feelings, even if they don’t say it out loud.

The good news is that uncertainty can be managed. You can learn to reduce anxiety, build confidence, and slowly find your balance again. Here are some simple ways to face uncertainty with strength.

1. Take Control of What Is in Your Control

Uncertainty grows bigger when the mind focuses on things it cannot control.
But there is always something—small or big—that is under your control.

For example:

  • Your daily routine
  • Your habits
  • Your effort
  • Your reactions
  • Your physical and mental self-care
  • The way you talk to yourself

Even during the Covid period, when the world felt stuck, many people found comfort by creating small habits that gave structure to their day. These habits don’t have to be perfect—just consistent.

Some simple habits that help:

  • Daily exercise or a short walk
  • Changing out of pajamas
  • Cooking a new recipe
  • Journaling or writing your thoughts
  • Meditation or breathing exercises
  • Limiting screen time

Habits create predictability. And predictability reduces fear.

2. Challenge the Uncertainty

Uncertainty often forces people to make decisions even when the path is not clear.
For example:

  • Changing a job
  • Moving to a new place
  • Starting something new
  • Ending something old
  • Investing money
  • Choosing a long-term plan

Challenging uncertainty does not mean acting without thinking.
It means taking careful steps, making informed choices, and accepting that not everything can be known in advance.

When dealing with uncertainty, remember:

  • Gather as much information as you can
  • Think long-term
  • Prepare backup options
  • Give yourself time
  • Avoid rushing
  • Talk to someone you trust

Challenging uncertainty takes courage, but it also helps you grow.

3. Accept That Uncertainty Can’t Be Removed Completely

No matter how carefully life is planned, reality always includes unexpected situations.
Every day people accept small uncertainties without even thinking about them:

  • Crossing a road
  • Driving a car
  • Eating in a restaurant
  • Taking a flight
  • Going to work or school
  • Meeting new people

In all these cases, there is a tiny risk. Yet life continues, because the mind learns to trust and move forward.

In the same way, bigger uncertainties also need acceptance.
You don’t need a 100% guarantee to live your life.
You just need enough trust to take the next step.

Acceptance doesn’t mean giving up. It means understanding that not everything can be predicted—and that’s okay.

4. Focus on the Present Situation

The mind usually fears future possibilities, not present reality.
Most anxiety comes from imagining a worst-case scenario that has not even happened.

A simple way to calm the mind is by asking:

“How am I right now?”
“What is actually happening in this moment?”

Usually, the present moment is much safer than the future your mind is imagining.

To stay present:

  • Take a few deep breaths
  • Observe your surroundings
  • Feel your body and your breath
  • Reduce negative news
  • Switch off unnecessary alerts
  • Spend more time in nature
  • Practice mindfulness

When attention stays in the present, the fear of “What if?” loses its power.

5. Build Emotional Strength Slowly

Facing uncertainty becomes easier when emotional strength increases.
Some ways to build it are:

  • Talking to a trusted friend
  • Sharing your worries instead of holding them inside
  • Getting enough sleep
  • Eating healthy
  • Taking small breaks
  • Practicing gratitude
  • Setting small, achievable goals

Every small improvement adds up. Over time, your inner confidence becomes stronger than the uncertainty outside.

Conclusion

Uncertainty is something everyone faces. It might shake the mind, but it does not have to control your life.
By focusing on what you can control, challenging uncertainty with caution, accepting unpredictability, and staying grounded in the present, you can reduce anxiety and bring more peace into your daily life.

Life becomes lighter when you stop fighting uncertainty and start learning how to live with it.
