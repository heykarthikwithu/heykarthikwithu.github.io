Bangalore to Savandurga Rock Trek – Solo Trip Plan

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Savandurga, known as one of the largest monolith hills in Asia, is a popular trekking destination located just 60 km from Bangalore. With its rugged trails, panoramic views, and historical ruins, it offers an ideal solo trekking experience for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts alike. Whether you're seeking a quick escape or a physical challenge, this guide will help you plan your solo adventure to Savandurga with ease.

Route from Bangalore to Savandurga Rock

The distance from Bangalore to Savandurga is about 60 km, and the journey takes around 1.5 to 2 hours depending on traffic.

  • Starting Point: Bangalore
  • Route: Bangalore → Mysore Road → Dodda Aladamara Road → Savandurga
  • Travel Modes:
    • By Bike/Car: Ideal for solo travelers looking for flexibility and scenic detours.
    • By Bus: Buses are available from Bangalore to Magadi; from there, take a local auto or cab to Savandurga.

Itinerary Summary

  • 5:30 a.m.: Start early from Bangalore to avoid the heat and crowds.
  • 7:00 a.m.: Arrive at the base of Savandurga hill.
  • 7:15 a.m.: Begin the trek via the Karigudda or Biligudda trails.
  • 9:30 a.m.: Reach the summit and enjoy stunning views of the Arkavathi river, forests, and villages.
  • 10:30 a.m.: Begin the descent.
  • 12:00 p.m.: Return to the base and refresh.
  • 12:30 p.m.: Visit nearby Manchinbele Dam for a relaxing break.
  • 2:00 p.m.: Have lunch at a local dhaba or return to Bangalore for lunch.
  • 4:00 p.m.: Reach Bangalore by evening.

What to Expect

  • Moderate-Level Trek: A 2 to 3-hour uphill trek with rocky patches and steep climbs.
  • Stunning Views: Panoramic landscapes of the Arkavathi River and surrounding forests.
  • Peaceful Vibe: Ideal for solo travelers seeking solitude and a sense of achievement.
  • Historical Elements: Ruins of an old fort and temple near the summit.

On the Way: Places to Visit

  • Dodda Alada Mara (Big Banyan Tree): A 400-year-old banyan tree—perfect for a short nature stop.
  • Manchinbele Dam: A serene reservoir great for relaxing and photography.
  • Thippagondanahalli Reservoir: A quiet water body en route to Savandurga.
  • Sri Savandi Veerabhadreshwara Temple: Located at the base of the hill, worth a quick visit.

Best Time to Visit

The best time for a Savandurga trek is between October and February, when the weather is cool and dry.

  • Winter (Oct-Feb): Ideal for trekking with pleasant weather and clear views.
  • Monsoon (Jun-Sep): Trekking can be risky due to slippery rocks—avoid during rains.
  • Summer (Mar-May): Very hot—if trekking, start very early to avoid midday heat.

Travel Tips for a Smooth Solo Trip

  • Start Early: Begin before sunrise to enjoy the trek in cooler temperatures.
  • Wear Proper Gear: Sturdy trekking shoes, a cap, and sunglasses are essential.
  • Carry Essentials: Water, snacks, a basic first-aid kit, and sunscreen.
  • Stay Safe: Avoid isolated spots during the descent and inform someone about your plans.
  • No Facilities on the Hill: There are no restrooms or shops—prepare accordingly.
  • Respect Nature: Don’t litter and follow marked paths for safety.

Conclusion

A solo trek to Savandurga is the perfect blend of adventure, nature, and solitude. Whether you’re a seasoned trekker or a first-timer, the experience of climbing one of Asia’s largest monoliths is both empowering and enriching. So pack your gear, rise with the sun, and head out on a memorable solo journey to Savandurga
