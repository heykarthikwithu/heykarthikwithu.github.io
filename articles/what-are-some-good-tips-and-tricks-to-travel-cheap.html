What are some good tips and tricks to travel cheap?

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Traveling on a budget can be both rewarding and challenging.

Here are some tips and tricks to help you travel cheaply while still having an enriching and enjoyable experience:

Planning and Preparation

  • Flexible Travel Dates:
    • Being flexible with your travel dates can help you find cheaper flights and accommodations.
    • Use tools like Google Flights or Skyscanner to compare prices over a range of dates.
  • Travel Off-Peak:
    • Traveling during the off-peak season can significantly reduce costs for flights, accommodations and attractions.
    • Research the best times to visit your destination to take advantage of lower prices.
  • Set a Budget:
    • Determine how much you can afford to spend and create a budget.
    • Allocate funds for accommodation, food, transportation, activities and emergencies.

Flights and Transportation

  • Use Flight Comparison Sites: Websites like Skyscanner, Kayak and Momondo allow you to compare flight prices across different airlines and booking platforms to find the best deals.
  • Book in Advance: While last-minute deals can sometimes be cheaper, booking flights and accommodations in advance often ensures better prices and availability.
  • Consider Alternative Airports:
    • Flying into or out of nearby airports can sometimes be cheaper than major hubs.
    • Check for budget airlines and alternative routes.
  • Public Transportation:
    • Use public transportation instead of taxis or rideshares.
    • Buses, trains and subways are often much cheaper and give you a more authentic experience of the destination.

Accommodation

  • Stay in Hostels or Budget Hotels:
    • Hostels and budget hotels are cost-effective options.
    • Websites like Hostelworld and Booking.com offer a range of budget accommodations.
  • Use Accommodation Platforms:
    • Platforms like Airbnb and Couchsurfing can provide affordable and unique lodging options.
    • Couchsurfing also allows you to stay with locals for free, which can enhance your cultural experience.
  • House Sitting: Stay in someone’s home for free in exchange for taking care of their house and pets, some websites provide this options.

Food and Dining

  • Cook Your Own Meals:
    • If your accommodation has a kitchen, buy groceries and cook your own meals.
    • This can save a significant amount of money compared to eating out.
  • Eat Like a Local:
    • Avoid touristy restaurants and eat where the locals eat.
    • Street food, local markets and small eateries often offer delicious food at a fraction of the cost.
  • Picnic:
    • Pack a picnic with items from a local grocery store or market.
    • Enjoying a meal in a park or by the beach can be both cheap and memorable.

Activities and Sightseeing

  • Free Attractions: Look for free attractions and activities such as parks, museums with free admission days, walking tours and cultural events.
  • Discount Passes:
    • Many cities offer tourist passes that provide discounts on attractions and public transportation.
    • Research and invest in these passes if they offer good value.
  • DIY Tours:
    • Instead of booking expensive guided tours, create your own itinerary and explore at your own pace.
    • Use online resources and travel apps to learn about the destinations.

Money Management

  • Avoid Currency Exchange Fees: Use credit cards with no foreign transaction fees and withdraw local currency from ATMs to avoid high exchange rates and fees at currency exchange booths.
  • Travel Insurance: While it might seem like an extra expense, travel insurance can save you money in case of emergencies, cancellations, or lost belongings.

General Tips

  • Pack Light:
    • Travel with carry-on luggage to avoid checked baggage fees and make transportation easier.
    • Packing light also allows for more flexibility and mobility.
  • Volunteer: Consider volunteering through organizations. In exchange for a few hours of work per day, you can receive free accommodation and sometimes meals.
  • Connect with Locals: Use events to connect with locals who can offer insider tips, recommend affordable places and even show you around.

By incorporating these tips and tricks, you can make your travels more affordable while still experiencing all the adventure and cultural richness your destinations have to offer. Happy travels.
