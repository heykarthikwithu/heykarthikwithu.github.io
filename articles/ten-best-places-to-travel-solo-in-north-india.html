Ten best places to Travel Solo in North India?

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Here are ten of the best places to travel solo in North India, along with suggestions on when to visit and why they are ideal for solo travelers:

  • Leh-Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir
    • When: May to September (summer months)
    • Why: During this time, the weather is favorable and most roads and passes are open, allowing solo travelers to explore the stunning landscapes, serene monasteries and adventurous activities like trekking and motorbiking.
  • Rishikesh, Uttarakhand
    • When: Throughout the year, excluding monsoon season (July to September)
    • Why: Rishikesh offers a perfect blend of spirituality and adventure. Solo travelers can indulge in yoga, meditation, river rafting and trekking while immersing themselves in the tranquil environment along the Ganges River.
  • Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
    • When: March to June and October to November
    • Why: Shimla's pleasant weather, colonial architecture and scenic beauty make it an ideal destination for solo travelers. They can explore the historic attractions, go trekking in the surrounding hills and enjoy the charming ambiance of the hill station.
  • Dharamshala-McLeodGanj, Himachal Pradesh
    • When: March to June and September to November
    • Why: With its Tibetan Buddhist culture, serene atmosphere and stunning views of the Dhauladhar Range, Dharamshala and McLeodGanj offer solo travelers an opportunity for spiritual retreat, trekking and volunteering in community activities.
  • Jaipur, Rajasthan
    • When: October to March (winter months)
    • Why: The Pink City of Jaipur boasts magnificent forts, palaces and vibrant bazaars. Solo travelers can explore the city's rich history, indulge in Rajasthani cuisine and immerse themselves in the colorful culture during the cooler months.
  • Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
    • When: October to March
    • Why: Varanasi, the oldest living city in the world, offers solo travelers a unique spiritual experience. They can witness ancient rituals along the ghats of the Ganges, explore narrow lanes filled with temples and markets and delve into the city's rich cultural heritage.
  • Amritsar, Punjab
    • When: October to March
    • Why: Home to the iconic Golden Temple, Amritsar offers solo travelers a chance to experience Sikh spirituality, hospitality and culinary delights. They can participate in langar (community kitchen), witness the Wagah Border ceremony and explore the city's historical landmarks.
  • Udaipur, Rajasthan
    • When: October to March
    • Why: Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is famous for its romantic ambiance, stunning palaces and tranquil lakes. Solo travelers can explore the city's architectural marvels, take boat rides on Lake Pichola and enjoy the royal hospitality of the region.
  • Nainital, Uttarakhand
    • When: March to June and September to November
    • Why: Surrounded by lush green hills and dotted with lakes, Nainital offers solo travelers a peaceful retreat amidst nature. They can enjoy boating on Naini Lake, trekking in the nearby hills and exploring colonial-era buildings and markets.
  • Manali, Himachal Pradesh
    • When: March to June and September to November
    • Why: Nestled in the Himalayas, Manali is a paradise for solo travelers seeking adventure and tranquility. They can engage in trekking, river rafting, paragliding and skiing (during winters), while soaking in the scenic beauty of the mountains.

These destinations offer unique experiences and diverse landscapes, catering to the interests of solo travelers throughout the year. Whether you seek spiritual enlightenment, cultural immersion, or adrenaline-pumping adventures, North India has something to offer for every solo traveler.
