Bangalore to Makalidurga Trek: One-Day Trekking Guide for Couples, Friends, and Families

By Karthik Kumar D K | Published: Dec 3, 2025 3:16 PM IST
By Karthik Kumar D K , 3 December 2025

Makalidurga is one of the most rewarding and beginner-friendly trekking destinations near Bangalore. Located just 60 km from the city, this beautiful hill offers a perfect one-day getaway for bikers, couples, families, and anyone looking to experience nature without venturing too far. With its ancient hill fort, scenic Gundamagere Lake, railway track crossing, and monsoon greenery, Makalidurga is an ideal choice if you want a short yet memorable trekking adventure.

About Makalidurga: A Short Introduction

Makalidurga is a historic hill fort located in Doddaballapur taluk, around 60 km north of Bangalore. The hill rises to a height of about 1,350 meters above sea level and is surrounded by greenery and rocky terrain. At the summit, you’ll find the remnants of an old fort and a small Shiva temple, believed to be the place where Markandeya Rishi performed penance centuries ago.

The hill is managed by the Karnataka Forest Department, and trekking is only allowed with an official online ticket. Illegal entry is strictly prohibited.

Why Makalidurga is Ideal for a One-Day Trip from Bangalore

  • Close to Bangalore (barely 1.5 hours away)
  • Easy to reach by bike or car
  • Safe for couples, families, and beginners
  • Trek is short (approx. 4 km total, up and down)
  • Stunning views of Gundamagere Lake
  • Experiencing sunrise here is memorable
  • Perfect year-round, especially during monsoon and winter

If you are looking for a quick break from the city crowd, Makalidurga gives you fresh air, adventure, and peace without a long drive.

Bangalore to Makalidurga Route Overview

Route:
Bangalore → Yelahanka → Doddaballapur → Makalidurga Parking → Trek Starting Point → Makalidurga Summit → Bangalore
This route is straightforward and suitable for two-wheelers, cars, and families with children.

Section 1: Bangalore → Makalidurga Parking

Distance: 60 km
Travel Time: 1 hour 15 minutes (including a small tea break)

1. Best Time to Start

Leaving Bangalore early morning is highly recommended.

  • Start at 4:30 AM or 5:00 AM
  • Reach the parking area before sunrise (~6:00 AM)

The weather will be cool, and you avoid traffic on the way.

2. Route Details

Drive towards Yelahanka and continue straight to Doddaballapur town. Once you cross Doddaballapur, continue for about 14 km until you see a left turn leading to Makalidurga.
This left leads you into a mud road for around 500 meters, and soon you will reach the designated parking area.

3. Parking

A basic parking area is provided by the Forest Department at the base of Makalidurga hill. It is safe to park both bikes and cars here.

Section 2: Makalidurga Parking → Trek Starting Point

From the parking area, the trek starting point is nearby.

How to Find the Starting Point

  • Walk straight ahead from the parking
  • Cross the railway track carefully
  • Take a left after the railway track

A forest officer will check your online permit at this point.
Note: Trekking without a valid ticket is prohibited.

Section 3: Trek Starting Point → Makalidurga Summit

The real adventure begins here. The trek combines rocky pathways, shrub patches, granite boulders, and scenic viewpoints.

1. Initial Trail

Within the first 100 meters, you will notice Muthuraya Swamy temple on your right side. Continue straight from here until you see a clear deviation.

At this point:

  • Right trail → Leads to Makalidurga hilltop
  • Left trail → Goes toward Gundamagere Lake

Follow the right trail to start ascending the hill.

2. Trail Markings

The entire route is marked with white arrows painted on rocks. Follow them to stay on the right path.

3. Difficulty Level

The trek is short but involves:

  • Rocky surfaces
  • Slippery granite slopes (especially during monsoons)
  • Steep sections

Beginners can do it easily with slow and careful steps.

Key Points Along the Trek

1. Moderate Rocky Ascent

The trail becomes steeper after 15–20 minutes. You may need to take small breaks.

2. First Viewpoint

After crossing the first steep section, you get a beautiful panoramic view of Gundamagere Lake, especially during sunrise.
This is a great place to take photos or rest for a minute.

3. Slippery Granite Rock Section

The next section contains a large granite rock that can be slippery when wet. Walk slowly and avoid stepping on moss.

4. Natural Rest Point

After the slippery section, you will reach a small open flat rock area. This is an ideal place to take your first long break.
From here, you can see the hilltop clearly. The summit is only about 20 minutes away.

5. Final Ascent

The last stretch is slightly steep but well-marked. After a short climb, you reach the fort entrance, marking the end of the ascent.

At the Summit: What to Expect

Once you reach the top, you will find:

1. Ruined Hill Fort

The old fort walls still stand strong, showing traces of history. You can walk along the fort’s boundary for better views.

2. Small Shiva Temple

A peaceful temple dedicated to Shiva and Nandi sits at the summit. Locals believe Markandeya Rishi meditated here.

3. Panoramic Views

You get 360-degree views of:

  • Gundamagere Lake
  • Surrounding hills
  • Railway track
  • Farmlands
  • Thick greenery during monsoon

Spend at least 30 to 60 minutes enjoying the silence and scenery before descending.

Section 4: Descent from Summit → Parking Area

Descending takes about 1 hour to 1 hour 30 minutes depending on your pace.

Important Tips for Descent

  • The steep granite rock section requires extra care.
  • Use your hands if needed for balance.
  • Better to descend slowly than to rush and slip.

Rest again at the natural flat rock area if needed.

Once you reach the base, cross the railway track and walk back to the parking area.

Food and Refreshments

There are no food stalls near the parking area.

Best Options:

  • Restaurants in Doddaballapur
  • Breakfast stops on the way back to Bangalore
  • Carry energy bars, fruits, and at least 2 liters of water

Ideal One-Day Itinerary Summary

4:45 AM – Start from Bangalore
6:00 AM – Reach Makalidurga parking
6:15 AM – Start the trek
7:30–8:00 AM – Reach the summit
8:00–9:00 AM – Explore the fort and temple
9:00–10:15 AM – Descend
10:30 AM – Drive to Doddaballapur for breakfast
12:00 PM – Drive back to Bangalore
1:00 PM – Reach home

Perfect for a half-day adventure.

Tips for Couples, Families, and Riders

1. For Couples on Bike

  • Carry extra water
  • Wear trekking shoes
  • Avoid returning in the afternoon heat

2. For Families in a Car

  • Suitable for children above 8 years
  • Start early to avoid crowd and sun

3. For Riders

Because tiredness hits during return:

  • If possible, have two riders to share the return journey
  • Take breaks at regular intervals
  • Wear gloves and knee guards for safety

Makalidurga Trek: Final Thoughts

Makalidurga is one of the most accessible and enjoyable treks near Bangalore. It offers adventure, history, nature, and breath-taking views all packed into a short one-day trip. With a simple route, safe trekking paths, and beautiful scenery, this trek is perfect for beginners, couples, families, and anyone needing a quick escape from city life.

If you’re planning a short getaway from Bangalore, Makalidurga is one of the best options for a refreshing morning trek.
