Bangalore to Kolli Hills Solo Nature Retreat – Road Trip Guide for Riders and Nature Lovers

By Karthik Kumar D K | Published: Jun 19, 2026 12:59 PM IST
By Karthik Kumar D K , 19 June 2026
Route map from Bangalore to Kolli Hills

If you are looking for a peaceful escape from Bangalore that combines scenic mountain roads, dense forests, waterfalls, viewpoints, hairpin bends, and a refreshing climate, then a Bangalore to Kolli Hills solo nature retreat is one of the most rewarding road trips you can experience in South India.

Unlike commercial hill stations such as Ooty or Kodaikanal, Kolli Hills remains relatively untouched by mass tourism. Nestled in the Eastern Ghats of Tamil Nadu, this hidden gem offers solitude, natural beauty, winding mountain roads, and an opportunity to disconnect from city life.

Whether you are a solo motorcycle rider seeking adventure, a photographer chasing landscapes, or simply someone looking to spend a weekend surrounded by nature, Kolli Hills delivers a memorable experience.

A two-day trip is ideal, though adventure riders often complete it as a long one-day ride. The route itself is enjoyable, featuring smooth highways, countryside scenery, and the famous 70 hairpin bends that lead to the hilltop.

Below is a complete travel guide, route plan, places to visit, road conditions, and itinerary for your Bangalore to Kolli Hills solo nature retreat.

About Kolli Hills

Kolli Hills, locally known as Kollimalai, is situated in the Namakkal district of Tamil Nadu at an altitude of approximately 1,300 meters above sea level.

The hill range is famous for:

  • 70 Hairpin Bends
  • Agaya Gangai Waterfalls
  • Arapaleeswarar Temple
  • Dense forests
  • Panoramic viewpoints
  • Pleasant climate
  • Less crowded atmosphere
  • Traditional tribal villages

Unlike many tourist destinations that have become heavily commercialized, Kolli Hills still retains its natural charm and peaceful environment.

For solo travelers looking to recharge mentally and physically, Kolli Hills is one of the best nature retreats within riding distance from Bangalore.

Route

Bangalore → Hosur → Krishnagiri → Salem → Rasipuram → Senthamangalam → Kolli Hills

Distance

Approximately 270–300 km one way depending on your starting location in Bangalore.

Travel Time

6 to 7 hours including fuel and breakfast breaks.

Bangalore to Salem

Leaving Bangalore around 4:30 AM or 5:00 AM is highly recommended.

The Bangalore-Chennai Highway till Krishnagiri offers excellent road conditions and smooth riding.

After Krishnagiri, continue towards Salem.

This section of the journey is mostly highway riding, making it comfortable for both motorcycles and cars.

You will find numerous fuel stations, restaurants, and tea shops throughout the route.

Breakfast Stop

Salem is one of the best places to stop for breakfast.

You can take a short break, refuel yourself and your motorcycle, and prepare for the climb towards Kolli Hills.

The roads after Salem become more scenic as urban landscapes gradually give way to countryside views.

Salem to Kolli Hills

From Salem, proceed towards Rasipuram and Senthamangalam.

The roads are generally in good condition and pass through villages, farms, and lush greenery.

Once you reach the foothills, the real adventure begins.

The Famous 70 Hairpin Bends

The highlight of every Kolli Hills journey is the ascent through 70 hairpin bends.

The road is well maintained and offers spectacular views at every turn.

As you climb higher, you'll notice:

  • Cooler temperatures
  • Thick vegetation
  • Valley views
  • Fresh mountain air
  • Scenic photography opportunities

Each hairpin bend is numbered, making the climb even more interesting.

Motorcycle enthusiasts often consider this one of the best riding roads in South India.

Take your time during the climb and enjoy the experience rather than rushing.

Reaching Kolli Hills

You will likely reach Kolli Hills around noon if you leave Bangalore early.

The atmosphere changes dramatically from the busy city environment.

The air becomes cleaner, traffic nearly disappears, and nature surrounds you in every direction.

Check into your accommodation and spend some time relaxing before beginning local exploration.

Seeku Parai View Point

One of the first places you should visit is Seeku Parai View Point.

This viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of the valleys below.

On clear days, you can see vast stretches of Tamil Nadu's plains extending into the distance.

Sunrise and sunset are particularly beautiful here.

Photographers can spend hours capturing the changing colors of the landscape.

Arapaleeswarar Temple

The ancient Arapaleeswarar Temple is one of the most important landmarks in Kolli Hills.

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is believed to be centuries old and attracts devotees from across Tamil Nadu.

Even if you are not particularly religious, the temple's peaceful setting and architecture make it worth visiting.

The temple also serves as the starting point for the trek to Agaya Gangai Waterfalls.

Agaya Gangai Waterfalls

No trip to Kolli Hills is complete without visiting Agaya Gangai Waterfalls.

This is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Tamil Nadu.

Trek Details

To reach the waterfall, visitors must descend approximately 1,000 steps.

The trek requires moderate fitness.

The journey down is relatively easy, but the climb back can be physically demanding.

However, the reward is worth every step.

The waterfall cascades dramatically from a height of around 300 feet and is surrounded by lush forest.

The roaring sound of water, cool mist, and untouched natural surroundings create a truly unforgettable experience.

Carry drinking water and wear comfortable footwear.

Boat House

Kolli Hills also has a small boating facility surrounded by greenery.

It is a peaceful place to spend some time during the afternoon.

Families and couples often enjoy this area, while solo travelers appreciate the calm atmosphere.

Nature Walks and Forest Exploration

One of the greatest advantages of Kolli Hills is the opportunity to simply walk and explore.

Unlike crowded hill stations, many roads here remain quiet and peaceful.

You can enjoy:

  • Forest roads
  • Village trails
  • Scenic viewpoints
  • Coffee plantations
  • Pepper farms

These walks often become the most memorable part of the trip.

Local Food

Food options in Kolli Hills are simple but satisfying.

You can find:

  • South Indian meals
  • Dosas
  • Idlis
  • Parottas
  • Chicken dishes
  • Fresh fruits

Do not expect luxury dining experiences.

Instead, enjoy authentic local cuisine served by small family-run establishments.

Best Time to Visit Kolli Hills

1. October to February

The best season for visiting Kolli Hills.

Pleasant weather, clear skies, and comfortable temperatures make outdoor activities enjoyable.

2. June to September

Monsoon transforms the hills into a green paradise.

Waterfalls become more powerful, and forests appear vibrant.

However, roads may become slippery due to rain.

Suggested Two-Day Itinerary

Day 1

  • 4:30 AM – Leave Bangalore
  • 8:00 AM – Breakfast near Salem
  • 11:30 AM – Reach Kolli Hills
  • 1:00 PM – Check into accommodation
  • 2:00 PM – Seeku Parai View Point
  • 4:00 PM – Local exploration

  • 8:00 PM – Dinner and overnight stay

  • 6:00 AM – Sunrise
  • 7:00 AM – Breakfast

  • 9:00 AM – Trek to Agaya Gangai Waterfalls
  • 1:00 PM – Lunch
  • 2:00 PM – Begin return journey

Solo Rider Tips

  • Start early to avoid city traffic.
  • Carry rain gear throughout the year.
  • Maintain safe speeds on hairpin bends.
  • Avoid riding after dark in remote forest sections.
  • Keep sufficient fuel before climbing the hills.
  • Download offline maps as network coverage can be inconsistent.
  • Inform family or friends about your travel plan.

Accommodation Options

Kolli Hills offers:

  • Budget lodges
  • Homestays
  • Eco resorts
  • Farm stays

For solo travelers, homestays often provide the best experience because they offer local insights and a peaceful atmosphere.

Advance booking is recommended during weekends and holiday periods.

Why Kolli Hills is Perfect for a Solo Nature Retreat

Modern life is often filled with noise, deadlines, traffic, and constant digital distractions.

Kolli Hills offers something increasingly rare: silence.

The winding roads, forested landscapes, cool climate, and minimal crowds create an environment where you can genuinely slow down and reconnect with nature.

Whether you spend your time riding through mountain roads, watching clouds drift across valleys, trekking to waterfalls, or simply sitting quietly at a viewpoint, Kolli Hills has a unique ability to refresh the mind.

For solo travelers from Bangalore seeking an affordable, scenic, and less-commercialized weekend getaway, Kolli Hills remains one of South India's best hidden treasures.

Conclusion

A Bangalore to Kolli Hills solo nature retreat is much more than a simple road trip. It is an opportunity to escape urban chaos, experience one of Tamil Nadu's most beautiful hill ranges, and enjoy a journey filled with forests, waterfalls, mountain roads, and unforgettable viewpoints.

From the thrilling 70 hairpin bends to the magnificent Agaya Gangai Waterfalls, every part of the journey offers something unique. Whether you ride a motorcycle, drive a car, or simply seek a peaceful weekend surrounded by nature, Kolli Hills is a destination that deserves a place on your travel bucket list.

Pack your riding gear, fuel up your vehicle, leave the city behind, and discover why Kolli Hills continues to be one of South India's most rewarding nature retreats.
