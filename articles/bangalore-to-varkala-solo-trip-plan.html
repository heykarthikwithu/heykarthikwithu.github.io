Bangalore to Varkala Solo Trip: Beach & Travel Guide

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

A solo trip from Bangalore to Varkala is a perfect way to unwind and recharge. Varkala, a picturesque coastal town in Kerala, is famous for its cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, pristine beaches, backwaters, and spiritual significance. This journey combines scenic drives through the Western Ghats, serene beach experiences, cultural insights, and relaxation.

Whether you are a solo traveler seeking adventure or peace, this itinerary is designed to make your trip smooth, memorable, and enjoyable.

Route Options from Bangalore to Varkala

Depending on your preference, you can choose from the following routes:

  1. Bangalore → Salem → Coimbatore → Trivandrum → Varkala → Madurai → Salem → Bangalore
  2. Bangalore → Mysore → Ooty → Coimbatore → Trivandrum → Varkala → Madurai → Salem → Bangalore

Both routes offer scenic landscapes, with the second route passing through the Nilgiri Hills around Ooty, adding hill station charm to your journey. The trip spans approximately 680 km and takes around 12–13 hours by road, making early departure essential.

Day 1: Bangalore to Varkala

Start your journey early in the morning, preferably by 5:00 AM. Leaving early will help you avoid city traffic and enjoy a calm ride or drive.

Scenic Drive Highlights

  • Western Ghats: Enjoy lush greenery, winding roads, and misty hills if you take the route via Ooty.
  • Coimbatore: A major city en route, perfect for a quick breakfast or coffee break.
  • Trivandrum: The capital of Kerala, known for its cultural and historical landmarks. Stop briefly if you want to stretch your legs or grab refreshments.

Arrival in Varkala

You will reach Varkala by late evening. Check into your accommodation and freshen up.
In the evening, explore the Varkala Cliff, which is a vibrant stretch with cafes, shops, and restaurants overlooking the Arabian Sea. It’s a perfect spot for photography, enjoying local snacks, or simply relaxing while watching the waves crash against the cliffs.

Overnight Stay: Varkala

Day 2: Exploring Varkala

Varkala is more than just a beach town; it offers spiritual experiences, natural beauty, and adventure.

Morning Activities

  1. Varkala Beach: Start your day early with a visit to the beach. This beach is famous for its unique cliffs, golden sands, and calm waters. You can:
    1. Relax on the sands and enjoy the sunrise.
    2. Swim in the Arabian Sea.
    3. Try water sports like surfing, parasailing, or banana boat rides.
  2. Janardanaswamy Temple: A must-visit Hindu pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple has historical significance and showcases Kerala-style architecture. Devotees from across India visit for blessings and spiritual rejuvenation.
  3. Sivagiri Mutt: The final resting place of Sree Narayana Guru, a social reformer and spiritual leader. The ashram holds daily prayers, meditation sessions, and is an important site for understanding local history and spirituality.

Afternoon Activities

  1. Backwater Boat Ride: Take a relaxing boat ride through the backwaters of Varkala, surrounded by lush greenery and serene landscapes. This experience allows you to witness local village life and traditional Kerala architecture
  2. Additional Attractions:
    1. Kappil Lake: A peaceful lake where you can enjoy a short canoe or kayak ride.
    2. Anjengo Fort: Built by the British, this fort provides historical insights and panoramic views of the Arabian Sea.

Evening Activities

  • Witness the sunset from the cliffs—one of the most breathtaking experiences in Varkala.
  • Indulge in Ayurvedic treatments or yoga sessions offered by various wellness centers.

Overnight Stay: Varkala

Day 3: Beach Time and Departure

Spend your final morning soaking in the tranquil environment of Varkala.

  • Varkala Beach: Explore other sections like Papanasam Beach (believed to wash away sins) and Black Sand Beach, each offering unique experiences.
  • Varkala Tunnel: Visit the natural spring located near the cliffs, believed to have healing properties.

Departure to Bangalore

Begin your journey back in the afternoon, retracing your route via Trivandrum, Coimbatore, Salem, and back to Bangalore.
You will reach Bangalore by late evening, concluding a memorable solo trip.

Travel Tips for Solo Travelers

  1. Early Departure: Start as early as possible to cover the long distance comfortably.
  2. Road Conditions: Check highway and village road conditions; the Western Ghats route may have winding roads.
  3. Safety Gear: For motorbike riders, helmets, gloves, and reflective clothing are essential.
  4. Fuel & Breaks: Ensure full fuel tanks and take breaks every 2–3 hours to avoid fatigue.
  5. Navigation: Use GPS or Google Maps, especially for Kerala’s coastal and rural roads.
  6. Emergency Kit: Carry water, snacks, first-aid supplies, and your phone with a power bank.

Tips for Family Travelers

  1. Vehicle Preparation: Ensure your car is serviced for a long trip, especially brakes and tires.
  2. Comfortable Travel: Pack snacks, water, and entertainment for children.
  3. Hotel Booking: Book accommodation in advance near the cliffs or beach for easy access to attractions.
  4. Local Cuisine: Try Kerala delicacies like seafood, Kerala sadya, and coconut-based dishes at local eateries.
  5. Combine Sightseeing: Explore nearby attractions such as Anchuthengu, Kovalam, or Ponmudi hills if time permits.

Why Varkala is a Perfect Solo Travel Destination

  • Natural Beauty: Stunning cliffs, backwaters, and golden beaches.
  • Spiritual Significance: Janardanaswamy Temple and Sivagiri Mutt offer peace and insight into Kerala’s spiritual heritage.
  • Adventure Opportunities: Water sports and trekking along cliffs provide thrill for solo travelers.
  • Local Culture: Interact with locals, explore markets, and taste authentic Kerala cuisine.
  • Relaxation & Wellness: Ayurvedic treatments, yoga, and serene surroundings make it rejuvenating.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal months for a solo trip to Varkala are October to March, when the weather is pleasant for beach activities, sightseeing, and water sports. Avoid monsoon season (June–September) as heavy rains can make road travel challenging.

FAQs: Bangalore to Varkala Solo Trip

1. Is Varkala safe for solo travelers?
Yes. Varkala is one of Kerala’s most tourist-friendly coastal towns. The cliff area, beaches, and main roads are generally safe, especially during the tourist season. Still, solo travelers should avoid isolated areas late at night and follow basic travel precautions.

2. How many days are enough for a Varkala trip?
A 3-day itinerary is ideal. It gives enough time to explore the beaches, cliffs, Janardanaswamy Temple, Sivagiri Mutt, and enjoy a backwater experience without rushing.

3. What is the best time to visit Varkala?
October to March offers pleasant weather, calm seas, and ideal conditions for sightseeing and water activities.

4. Can beginners try water sports in Varkala?
Yes. Activities like surfing, kayaking, and parasailing are operated by certified instructors. Beginners can safely participate after basic instructions.

5. Are the roads good for a long drive or bike ride?
Yes. The highways from Bangalore through Salem and Coimbatore are smooth. Roads become narrower near coastal Kerala, but they are manageable with careful riding/driving.

Conclusion

A solo trip from Bangalore to Varkala offers the perfect mix of adventure, spirituality, relaxation, and scenic beauty. With a well-planned itinerary, you can enjoy the stunning beaches, cliffs, backwaters, and temples of this coastal town without any rush.

Whether it’s Varkala Beach, Janardanaswamy Temple, Sivagiri Mutt, or backwater rides, this itinerary ensures you make the most of your three-day journey while returning with unforgettable memories.
