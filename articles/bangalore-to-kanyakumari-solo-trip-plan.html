Bangalore to Kanyakumari Solo Trip Plan

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Solo trip from Bangalore to Kanyakumari can be a fascinating journey, as you'll get to experience the diverse landscapes, cultural heritage and the meeting point of the Arabian Sea, the Indian Ocean and the Bay of Bengal. Here's a suggested itinerary for your trip:

Route

Bangalore -> Salem -> Madurai -> Kanyakumari -> Salem -> Bangalore

Day 1: Bangalore to Madurai

  • Start your journey from Bangalore early in the morning.
  • The distance between Bangalore and Madurai is approximately 460 kilometers and it takes around 7-8 hours by road.
  • Take the route via Salem and Dindigul to reach Madurai.
  • Visit the famous Meenakshi Amman Temple, known for its intricate architecture and vibrant colors.
  • Explore the historic Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace and the Gandhi Memorial Museum.
  • In the evening, you can stroll around the vibrant streets of Madurai and savor some delicious local cuisine.
  • Overnight stay in Madurai.

Day 2: Madurai to Kanyakumari

  • Leave Madurai in the morning and head towards Kanyakumari.
  • The distance between Madurai and Kanyakumari is approximately 245 kilometers and it takes around 4-5 hours by road.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive as you pass through the beautiful landscapes of Tamil Nadu.
  • Upon reaching Kanyakumari, check into your accommodation and freshen up.
  • Visit the iconic Vivekananda Rock Memorial, located on a small island off the coast of Kanyakumari.
  • Explore the Thiruvalluvar Statue, dedicated to the Tamil poet and philosopher Thiruvalluvar.
  • Witness the breathtaking view of the sunset at the confluence of the three seas from the Kanyakumari Beach.
  • In the evening, you can take a leisurely walk along the coastline and enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Overnight stay in Kanyakumari.

Day 3: Exploring Kanyakumari

  • Start your day by witnessing the magnificent sunrise at Kanyakumari Beach.
  • Visit the Kumari Amman Temple, dedicated to the Goddess Kanyakumari.
  • Explore the Gandhi Mandapam, a memorial dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi and the adjacent Kanyakumari Memorial.
  • Take a ferry ride to the nearby Thiruvalluvar Statue and Vivekananda Rock Memorial, if you didn't get a chance to visit them on the previous day.
  • Visit the Padmanabhapuram Palace, a well-preserved wooden palace known for its architectural beauty.
  • In the evening, you can relax at the beach or indulge in some souvenir shopping.
  • Overnight stay in Kanyakumari.

Day 4: Kanyakumari to Bangalore

  • Start your return journey to Bangalore.
  • The distance between Kanyakumari and Bangalore is approximately 660 kilometers and it takes around 11-12 hours by road.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive back and you can make stops along the way at places like Nagercoil or Tirunelveli for refreshments.
  • Reach Bangalore by late evening or night, depending on the traffic and your pace.
  • Conclude your solo trip to Kanyakumari with beautiful memories.

Please note that this itinerary provides a general outline for your trip and you can modify it according to your preferences and the time you have available. Make sure to check the current travel guidelines, road conditions and weather forecasts before embarking on your journey. Enjoy your solo adventure to Kanyakumari.
