Edible Slime, science experiment for children

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Creating edible slime is a fun and safe science experiment for children, combining sensory play with a touch of culinary creativity.

Here's a step-by-step guide to making edible slime using simple ingredients:

Materials:

  1. Marshmallows (large or mini)
  2. Cornstarch
  3. Vegetable oil
  4. Food coloring (optional)
  5. Microwave-safe bowl
  6. Mixing spoons

Procedure:

  • Melt the Marshmallows:
    • Place a cup of marshmallows in a microwave-safe bowl.
    • Add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil to the bowl. This helps prevent the marshmallows from sticking too much.
    • Microwave the marshmallows for about 30 seconds, or until they are puffy and melted. Be careful, as the bowl might be hot.
  • Stir in Cornstarch:
    • Add 1/2 cup of cornstarch to the melted marshmallows.
    • Stir the mixture with a spoon until it begins to come together. It will be sticky at first.
  • If you want to add color to your slime, now is the time to add a few drops of food coloring. Stir well to mix the color evenly.
  • Knead the Slime:
    • Once the mixture starts to form a ball, dust a clean surface with a little more cornstarch.
    • Transfer the marshmallow mixture to the surface and knead it with your hands. Add more cornstarch as needed until the slime reaches a stretchy, non-sticky consistency.
  • Once the slime is smooth and stretchy, it’s ready to play with. Remember, this slime is edible, so it’s safe to taste.

Explanation:

This experiment demonstrates how different ingredients can interact to create new textures. The marshmallows provide a stretchy, gooey base, while the cornstarch acts as a thickening agent, reducing stickiness and giving the slime a more solid structure.

Tips:

  • Storage: Store the edible slime in an airtight container at room temperature. It might need a bit of kneading if it becomes too stiff after storage.
  • Variations: Try adding flavored gelatin powder for different colors and flavors.
  • Safety: While the slime is edible, it's still important to ensure that all materials used are safe to consume and that children wash their hands before and after playing with the slime.

Discussion Points:

  • States of Matter: Discuss how the slime behaves like a solid and a liquid, making it a non-Newtonian fluid.
  • Polymers: Explain that the melted marshmallows contain sugar polymers that stretch and interact with the cornstarch.
  • Sensory Exploration: Encourage children to describe the texture, stretchiness and smell of the slime, enhancing their sensory and descriptive skills.

Creating edible slime is a delightful way to engage children in a tactile, taste-safe science experiment, combining fun and learning in one gooey package
