Bangalore to Agumbe Solo Trip: Travel Itinerary & Tips

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Solo trip from Bangalore to Agumbe can be a thrilling adventure, as you'll get to explore the rainforests, waterfalls and scenic beauty of the Western Ghats in Karnataka. Agumbe is also known as the "Cherrapunji of South India" due to its high rainfall and lush greenery.

Here's a suggested itinerary for your trip:

Route

Bangalore -> Hassan -> Belur -> Sringeri -> Agumbe -> Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary -> Hiriyur -> Bangalore

Day 1: Bangalore to Agumbe

  • Start your trip early in the morning from Bangalore.
  • The distance between Bangalore and Agumbe is approximately 350 kilometers and it takes around 7-8 hours by road.
  • Take the route via Hassan, Belur, Sringeri to reach Agumbe.
  • Enjoy the scenic drive through the Western Ghats and the beautiful countryside of Karnataka.
  • Reach Agumbe by late afternoon or early evening.
  • Check into your accommodation and take some time to relax and freshen up.
  • In the evening, you can explore the nearby attractions like the Agumbe Sunset Point, which offers breathtaking views of the sunset over the Western Ghats.
  • Spend the night in Agumbe.

Day 2: Rainforest Exploration

  • Start your day by exploring the rainforests and wildlife of Agumbe.
  • Visit the Agumbe Rainforest Research Station, where you can learn about the rich biodiversity of the region and participate in nature trails or birdwatching activities.
  • Explore the Kudremukh National Park, known for its dense forests and varied wildlife.
  • Visit the Barkana Falls, one of the highest waterfalls in India, located near Agumbe.
  • If you're interested, you can also visit the Jogigundi Falls or the Onake Abbi Falls.
  • In the evening, you can relax amidst the natural beauty of Agumbe and enjoy the peaceful ambiance.
  • Spend another night in Agumbe.

Day 3: Departure

  • Start your day by visiting the nearby attractions in Agumbe.
  • Explore the Kunchikal Falls, the highest waterfall in India, located in the vicinity of Agumbe.
  • Visit the Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary, known for its diverse flora and fauna.
  • If time permits, you can also visit the Kundadri Hills, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • In the afternoon, start your journey back to Bangalore.
  • Reach Bangalore by late evening and conclude your solo trip to Agumbe with beautiful memories.

Please note that this itinerary provides a general outline for your trip and you can modify it according to your preferences and the time you have available. Make sure to check the current travel guidelines, road conditions and weather forecasts before embarking on your journey. Enjoy your solo adventure in Agumbe.
