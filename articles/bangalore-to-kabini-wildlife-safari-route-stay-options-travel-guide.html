Bangalore to Kabini Wildlife Safari – Route, Stay Options & Travel Guide

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

A trip from Bangalore to Kabini is one of the best short getaways for wildlife lovers, nature enthusiasts, couples, and families. Kabini, located on the banks of the Kabini River and close to the famous Nagarahole National Park, is a paradise for those who love forests, wildlife spotting, peaceful resorts, and photography.

This guide gives you a simple, detailed, and practical plan to help you make the most of your Bangalore to Kabini trip—whether it’s a weekend break or a quick nature escape.

Route Overview

Bangalore → Ramanagara → Channapatna → Maddur → Mandya → Mysore → Antharasanthe → Kabini
This is the most preferred and convenient route with smooth roads, plenty of food stops, and clear signboards along the way.

Distance and Travel Time

  • Total distance: ~210 km (one way)
  • Travel time: 4.5 to 5.5 hours
  • Best time to start: Early morning (5:00–6:00 AM) for a pleasant drive and to reach Kabini before lunch

The drive is scenic, especially once you cross Mysore. You’ll pass through farmlands, coconut groves, and forested stretches as you approach Kabini.

Day 1: Bangalore → Kabini

Start early from Bangalore to escape traffic and reach Kabini by late morning or noon. NH75 and NH275 are well-maintained highways with multiple stops for breakfast.

1. Recommended Breakfast Places on the Way

  • Kamat Lokaruchi, Ramanagara
  • A2B, Maddur
  • Shivalli Restaurant, Mandya

After Mysore, take the road towards HD Kote and follow signboards to Nagarahole / Kabini.

Once you reach Kabini, check into your resort. Kabini has many luxury, mid-range, and nature-stay options.

2. Where to Stay in Kabini

Kabini offers a range of stays depending on your budget:

  • Budget Stays
    • Kabini River Lodge (Jungle Lodges and Resorts) – Best for wildlife lovers
    • Local homestays near Antharasanthe
  • Mid-Range Stays
    • Waterwoods Lodge
    • Red Earth Kabini
    • Kabini Lake View Resort
  • Luxury Resorts
    • KAAV Safari Lodge
    • Evolve Back Kabini
    • The Serai Kabini

After check-in, rest and enjoy lunch at your resort.

3. Evening Nature Walk or Birdwatching

Most resorts in Kabini offer guided activities:

  • Nature walk
  • Riverside relaxation
  • Cycling around village trails
  • Birdwatching sessions

The evening in Kabini is very peaceful with pleasant breeze, forest sounds, and a calm riverbank environment. It’s a refreshing break from city life.

Enjoy dinner at your resort and rest early because the real thrill begins the next morning.

Day 2: Kabini Wildlife Safari

This is the highlight of your trip. Kabini is one of India’s top wildlife destinations and part of Nagarahole Tiger Reserve, known for:

  • Tigers
  • Leopards (Kabini is famous for black panther sightings)
  • Elephants
  • Indian Gaurs
  • Spotted Deer and Sambar
  • Wild Dogs (Dholes)
  • Crocodiles
  • Hundreds of bird species

You can choose between two types of safaris:

1. Kabini Jungle Safari Options

Jeep Safari

  • Higher chances of big-cat sightings
  • Enters the deep forest zone
  • Limited seats → Must book in advance (especially weekends)

Boat Safari

  • Conducted on Kabini River
  • Best for spotting elephants, crocodiles, and birds
  • Very scenic and relaxing

Safari Timings

  • Morning Safari: 6:00 AM – 9:00 AM
  • Evening Safari: 3:00 PM – 6:00 PM

Morning safaris have better chances for spotting tigers and leopards.

2. What to Expect During the Safari

A typical Kabini safari includes:

  • A forest guide and driver
  • Exploration of different forest zones
  • Multiple wildlife sightings
  • Time near backwaters (ideal for photography)
  • 2.5 to 3 hours total duration

If you’re lucky, you may spot the legendary Black Panther (Saya), though sightings are rare.

After the morning safari, return to your resort, enjoy breakfast, and relax.

Day 2 Afternoon: Kabini Backwaters and Relaxation

Spend your afternoon exploring:

1. Kabini Backwaters

A peaceful, scenic spot perfect for:

  • Photography
  • Short walks
  • Watching herds of elephants in summer

2. Tribal Village Visit (Optional)

Some resorts arrange guided visits to nearby tribal settlements to explore local culture and handicrafts.

3. Spa / Resort Activities

If you’re looking for relaxation:

  • Ayurvedic massages
  • Poolside rest
  • Hammock time under trees

Enjoy your resort dinner and rest for the night.

Day 3: Explore Nearby Attractions and Return to Bangalore

Start your day with a relaxed breakfast and a small nature walk. If you want more adventure, try an optional activity:

1. Optional Morning Activities

  • Coracle Ride (seasonal)
  • Birdwatching
  • Short cycling trail

Pack up by 10 AM.

2. Nearby Places to Visit Before Returning

Nagarahole National Park (Entry Gate Visit)
Even if you don’t go inside, the gate itself gives a beautiful forest vibe for photos.

Kabini Dam (Beechankuppe)
A quiet spot with river views and open landscapes.

HD Kote Local Market
Good place to buy:

  • Local honey
  • Handcrafted bamboo items
  • Spices

Start your return journey by early afternoon and reach Bangalore by evening.

Practical Tips for Kabini Travelers

1. Book Your Safari Early

Safari slots get filled quickly on weekends and holidays. Booking through your resort or Jungle Lodges is recommended.

2. Carry Essentials

  • Light jacket for early mornings
  • Cap and sunglasses
  • Water bottle
  • Camera/binoculars
  • Comfortable clothing

3. Follow Forest Rules

  • Do not shout during safaris
  • Do not feed animals
  • Do not get off the jeep/boat unless permitted

4. Best Time to Visit Kabini

  • October to May: Best for wildlife sightings
  • Summer (March–May): High elephant activity near backwaters
  • Monsoon (June–September): Lush greenery and ideal for nature lovers

5. Safety

Kabini is very safe for travelers, couples, families, and solo visitors. Resorts are secure, and forest authorities maintain strict rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is Kabini safe for solo travelers?

Yes. Resorts are safe and safari groups include guides and experts.

2. How much does Kabini safari cost?

Depending on the type:

  • Jeep Safari: ₹1,500 – ₹3,500 per person
  • Boat Safari: ₹1,000 – ₹2,000 per person
    (Luxury resorts have higher all-inclusive packages)

3. How many days are enough for Kabini?

A 2-night, 3-day trip is ideal to enjoy safaris and resort experiences.

4. Can I drive a sedan or hatchback to Kabini?

Yes. Roads are smooth and suitable for all vehicles.

5. Do I need to book safari online?

Most bookings happen through your resort. Some slots are available at the forest office, but they are limited.

Final Thoughts

A Bangalore to Kabini trip is one of the best weekend getaways for anyone who loves nature, wildlife, and peaceful surroundings. Whether you go with family, partner, friends, or even solo, the journey offers a mix of forest adventure, scenic landscapes, luxury stays, and memorable safari experiences.

With a little planning and the right start time, your Kabini wildlife safari trip will become a refreshing and unforgettable escape from city life.
