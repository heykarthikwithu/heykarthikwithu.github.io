Bangalore to Talakadu: Couple’s Motorcycle Ride & Travel Guide

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Talakadu, on the banks of the river Kaveri, is a fascinating blend of history, mythology, and natural beauty. Once a flourishing city with more than 30 temples, Talakadu is today partly buried under shifting sand dunes, earning it the name “The Desert of Karnataka.” For a couple riding out of Bangalore on a motorcycle, this trip is the perfect mix of adventure, romance, and heritage. The open highways, scenic countryside, and the historic ruins of Talakadu make it an unforgettable weekend ride.

Route from Bangalore to Talakadu

The distance from Bangalore to Talakadu is about 135 km, taking around 3.5 to 4 hours by motorcycle.

  • Route: Bangalore → Bidadi → Ramanagara → Channapatna → Maddur → Malavalli → Talakadu
    The ride offers smooth highways (Mysore Road) combined with charming rural stretches near Malavalli.

Itinerary Summary

  • 6:00 a.m.: Start from Bangalore to enjoy the cool morning ride.
  • 7:00 a.m.: Breakfast stop at a popular eatery in Bidadi (try the famous Bidadi Thatte Idli).
  • 10:00 a.m.: Arrive at Talakadu. Explore the temples buried under sand and learn about the legend of the Talakadu curse.
  • 1:00 p.m.: Have lunch near Talakadu or pack a simple meal for a riverside break.
  • 2:30 p.m.: Relax by the Kaveri River—perfect for couples to unwind in a serene natural setting.
  • 4:00 p.m.: Begin the return ride.
  • 7:30 p.m.: Reach Bangalore by evening.

What to Expect

  • Romantic Ride Experience: The open Mysore Road highway and countryside detours are perfect for a couple’s motorcycle trip.
  • Heritage Exploration: Visit temples like Vaidyanatheshwara, Arkeshwara, and Pathaleshwara, partly buried in sand.
  • Nature & Calm: Sit by the Kaveri riverbanks for some quiet couple-time.
  • Mystical Legends: Learn about the Talakadu curse that turned a flourishing city into sand dunes.

On the Way: Places to Visit

  • Ramanagara: Popularly called the “Sholay Hills,” great for a short break or quick photos.
  • Channapatna: Known as the “Toy Town of Karnataka” where you can pick up handcrafted wooden souvenirs.
  • Somanathapura (detour, 25 km from Talakadu): Visit the Chennakesava Temple, a fine example of Hoysala architecture.
  • Maddur: Try the famous Maddur Vada with hot filter coffee.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Ideal for pleasant riding weather and comfortable exploration of sand dunes and temples.
  • Monsoon (June–Sep): The surroundings are lush and green, but the sand may be tricky to walk on.
  • Summer (Apr–May): Can be hot and draining, less suitable for a couple motorcycle trip.

Travel Tips for a Smooth Trip

  • Ride Gear: Both riders should wear helmets, jackets, gloves, and proper shoes for comfort and safety.
  • Carry Water: Essential when exploring the sandy terrain of Talakadu.
  • Fuel Up: Ensure your motorcycle is tanked up before leaving Malavalli as fuel stations are sparse closer to Talakadu.
  • Pack Light: A small backpack with snacks, sunscreen, and a mat for a riverside rest will enhance the couple experience.
  • Stay Option (if extending): You can stay overnight at nearby resorts like Jungle Lodges in Talakadu or Somanathapura for a more relaxed couple’s getaway.

Conclusion

A couple’s motorcycle ride from Bangalore to Talakadu is more than just a road trip—it’s a shared adventure into history, legend, and scenic beauty. From the thrill of the highway ride to the peaceful moments by the Kaveri, Talakadu offers the perfect blend of exploration and togetherness. If you’re a couple who loves riding and discovering hidden stories, Talakadu is a must-do journey.
