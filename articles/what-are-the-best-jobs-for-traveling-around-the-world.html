What are the best jobs for traveling around the world?

ByKarthik Kumar D K | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

If you're looking to travel the world while earning an income, there are various jobs that offer the flexibility and opportunities to do so.

Here are some of the best jobs for travelers:

Remote and Freelance Jobs

  • Freelance Writing:
    • Pros: High demand for content creation, flexibility and ability to write about diverse topics including travel.
    • Platforms: Upwork, Freelancer, Fiverr, Contena.
  • Web Development and Programming:
    • Pros: High demand, good pay, flexibility and ability to work from anywhere with an internet connection.
    • Platforms: Upwork, Toptal, GitHub Jobs.
  • Graphic Design:
    • Pros: Creative work, flexibility and opportunities in various industries.
    • Platforms: 99designs, Dribbble, Behance.
  • Virtual Assistance:
    • Pros: Diverse tasks, flexibility and the opportunity to work with different clients.
    • Platforms: Belay, Time Etc, Zirtual.
  • Social Media Management:
    • Pros: Growing field, creative work and the ability to work from anywhere.
    • Platforms: Upwork, Freelancer, PeoplePerHour.
  • Online Teaching/Tutoring:
    • Pros: High demand for English and other subject teachers, flexible hours and rewarding work.
    • Platforms: VIPKid, iTalki, Preply.

Content Creation and Influencing

  • Blogging/Vlogging:
    • Pros: Potential for multiple income streams (ads, sponsorships, affiliate marketing), creative expression and the ability to build a personal brand.
    • Platforms: WordPress, YouTube, Instagram.
  • Travel Photography/Videography:
    • Pros: Creative work, potential for high earnings and the ability to sell photos and videos to stock agencies or directly to clients.
    • Platforms: Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, Getty Images.

On-the-Ground Opportunities

  • Tour Guide:
    • Pros: Opportunity to share your passion for travel and history, meet new people and earn tips.
    • Platforms: ToursByLocals, GetYourGuide, Viator.
  • Travel Consultant/Agent:
    • Pros: Help others plan their travel, access to industry discounts and flexible work environment.
    • Platforms: American Society of Travel Advisors (ASTA), Travel Leaders.
  • Hospitality Jobs:
    • Pros: Opportunity to work in different countries, meet people and often includes accommodation.
    • Examples: Jobs in hostels, hotels and resorts.

Specialized Skills and Certifications

  • Scuba Diving Instructor:
    • Pros: Work in beautiful locations, meet adventurous people and teach a skill you're passionate about.
    • Certification: PADI, SSI.
  • Ski Instructor:
    • Pros: Seasonal work in scenic locations, meet fellow ski enthusiasts and free access to ski facilities.
    • Certification: PSIA, CSIA.
  • Yoga Instructor:
    • Pros: Teach classes in beautiful locations, meet health-conscious people and improve your own practice.
    • Certification: Yoga Alliance.

Seasonal and Short-Term Jobs

  • Au Pair:
    • Pros: Live with a host family, earn a stipend and experience a new culture.
    • Platforms: AuPairWorld, Cultural Care Au Pair.
  • Cruise Ship Worker:
    • Pros: Travel to various destinations, food and accommodation provided and diverse job opportunities.
    • Platforms: Cruise Ship Jobs, Carnival Careers.
  • Farm Work/WWOOFing:
    • Pros: Opportunity to live and work in rural areas, free accommodation and meals and learn about sustainable farming.
    • Platforms: WWOOF, Workaway, HelpX.

Additional Tips

  • Networking: Join travel and digital nomad communities to find job opportunities and connect with like-minded individuals.
  • Skill Development: Continuously improve your skills and certifications to stay competitive in the job market.
  • Flexibility: Be open to different types of work and destinations to maximize your opportunities for earning while traveling.

By exploring these job options, you can find ways to sustain your travels and enrich your experiences around the world.
