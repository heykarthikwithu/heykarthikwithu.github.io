How to prepare Kulfi: Traditional Indian Ice Cream with Saffron, Cardamom & Nuts

ByReddamma D | read
Listen Pause Resume Stop

Kulfi is the quintessential Indian frozen dessert—rich, dense, and full of flavor. Unlike Western ice creams, kulfi is not whipped, which gives it a firm, creamy texture that slowly melts in your mouth. Flavored with aromatic saffron, fragrant cardamom, and crunchy nuts like pistachios and almonds, this age-old dessert is a favorite at Indian celebrations, street-side stalls, and summer feasts.

As an experienced home cook, I can tell you there’s nothing quite as satisfying as preparing kulfi from scratch. The slow simmering of milk, the natural infusion of spices, and the final freeze all contribute to its unforgettable flavor and nostalgia.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk (optional, for extra richness)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 8–10 saffron strands (soaked in 1 tbsp warm milk)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped pistachios
  • 2 tablespoons chopped almonds
  • Kulfi molds or small cups for setting

Method

Step 1: Reduce the Milk

  1. Pour the full-fat milk into a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai.
  2. Bring it to a boil, then simmer on low heat.
  3. Keep stirring every 5–7 minutes and scrape the sides of the pan to prevent burning.
  4. Let the milk reduce to about half its original volume. This slow reduction is the heart of an authentic kulfi and cannot be rushed.

Step 2: Add Flavors

  1. Add sugar and sweetened condensed milk (if using) and stir until dissolved.
  2. Mix in the soaked saffron milk, cardamom powder, and half of the chopped nuts.
  3. Continue simmering for another 5–10 minutes until the mixture thickens to a rabri-like consistency.

Step 3: Cool and Pour

  1. Turn off the heat and allow the mixture to cool to room temperature.
  2. Pour into kulfi molds, popsicle molds, or small steel tumblers.
  3. Sprinkle remaining chopped nuts on top for added texture and beauty.

Step 4: Freeze

  1. Cover the molds with foil or lids and freeze for at least 6–8 hours or overnight.
  2. To demold, run the mold under warm water for a few seconds, then gently pull out the kulfi.

Tips from My Kitchen

  • For Extra Creaminess: Add 1 tablespoon of milk powder while reducing the milk.
  • Nut Options: You can also include cashews or crushed rose petals for a royal touch.
  • Vegan Variation: Use almond or coconut milk and sweeten with jaggery syrup.
  • Serving Suggestion: Serve sliced on a plate, drizzled with falooda or rose syrup for a classic kulfi falooda experience.

Why Kulfi Stands Out

Kulfi is not just a dessert—it’s tradition in a mold. Its slow cooking method concentrates the flavors and gives it depth that no quick-fix ice cream can match. The blend of saffron and cardamom creates a delicate aroma, while the nuts offer both richness and crunch. It’s a treat that brings generations to the table, especially during hot summers or festivals like Holi and Diwali.

Conclusion

Homemade kulfi is a labor of love—and worth every minute. From the careful simmering of milk to the final frozen delight, every bite carries the soul of Indian culinary tradition. So go ahead and make this timeless dessert at home. Serve it to your loved ones and let it melt hearts, one creamy scoop at a time.
