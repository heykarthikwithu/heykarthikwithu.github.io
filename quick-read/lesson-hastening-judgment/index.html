The Lesson of Hastening the Judgment

Once upon a time, in a peaceful village surrounded by golden fields and gentle hills, there lived a wise old farmer named Raman. He was known throughout the village for his calm nature and thoughtful words. Whenever people faced problems, they often visited him for advice.

Raman lived with his only son, Arjun, and a beautiful white horse. The horse was famous across the region for its strength, speed, and beauty. Many wealthy merchants had offered large sums of money to buy it, but Raman always refused.

"This horse is not just an animal," he would say. "It is part of our family."

One morning, the villagers gathered near Raman's house with worried expressions.

His horse had disappeared.

The villagers reflected on his words.

From that day forward, whenever something unexpected happened, they remembered Raman's lesson. Instead of immediately calling an event good or bad, they learned to wait, observe, and trust that the full picture would reveal itself in time.

And so the village became a little wiser, a little calmer, and much more patient.

Moral of the Story

Do not hasten to judge events as good or bad. Life unfolds over time, and what seems like misfortune today may become a blessing tomorrow. Wisdom comes from patience, understanding, and waiting to see the whole story before making conclusions.
