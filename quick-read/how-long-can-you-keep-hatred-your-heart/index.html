How Long Can You Keep Hatred in Your Heart?

By Karthik Kumar D K | Published: Apr 25, 2026 9:12 AM IST
By Karthik Kumar D K , 25 April 2026
How Long Can You Keep Hatred in Your Heart?

A Moral Story About Letting Go.

In a quiet village surrounded by green fields and tall coconut trees, there lived two boys named Arjun and Kiran. They were best friends since childhood. Everyone in the village admired their bond. They played together, studied together, and even dreamed of building something big together when they grew up.

Arjun was calm and thoughtful. He always believed in patience and kindness. Kiran, on the other hand, was energetic and quick-tempered. He was loyal but often let his emotions control him.

Despite their differences, they were inseparable.

The Incident That Changed Everything

One summer afternoon, the boys were playing near the village well. It was their favorite place. They would sit on the stone edge, talk about their dreams, and throw pebbles into the water.

That day, they started arguing over something small—a cricket bat.

“I told you it’s mine!” Kiran shouted.

“No, Kiran, it’s not. You borrowed it from me last week,” Arjun replied calmly.

But Kiran wasn’t in the mood to listen. His anger grew stronger with every word.

“You always act like you’re right!” Kiran yelled.

Arjun tried to explain, but before he could finish, Kiran pushed him.

Arjun stumbled and fell to the ground. He wasn’t hurt badly, but the pain in his heart was deeper than any injury.

Without saying a word, Arjun got up and walked away.

That was the last time they spoke.

The Birth of Hatred

Days turned into weeks. Weeks turned into months.

Kiran expected Arjun to come back and apologize. But Arjun never did.

Instead of reflecting on his mistake, Kiran let his anger grow. Slowly, that anger turned into hatred.

“Why should I apologize?” Kiran would tell himself. “He thinks he’s better than me anyway.”

Every time he saw Arjun from a distance, his heart would fill with bitterness.

Arjun, meanwhile, felt hurt—but not angry. He missed his friend deeply. But he also believed that some wounds needed time to heal.

A Lesson from the Old Teacher

One day, the village school organized a special session with an old teacher named Master Dev. He was known for his wisdom and life lessons.

All the children gathered under a large banyan tree.

Master Dev looked at the children and asked,
“Tell me, how long can you carry a heavy stone in your hand?”

“A few minutes,” one child said.

“Maybe an hour,” said another.

“Not very long,” most agreed.

Master Dev smiled and picked up a small stone.

“If I hold this for a minute, it feels light,” he said.
“If I hold it for an hour, it starts to hurt.”
“But if I hold it all day… my hand will become numb with pain.”

The children listened carefully.

Then he asked,
“What do you think this stone represents?”

No one answered.

“It represents anger… hatred… and grudges,” he explained.

“When you hold onto them for a short time, it may not hurt much. But if you keep holding onto them for days, months, or years… they begin to damage you from the inside.”

Kiran sat quietly, staring at the ground.

The Realization

That night, Kiran couldn’t sleep.

The teacher’s words kept echoing in his mind.

“How long can you carry a heavy stone?”

He realized that for months, he had been carrying something much heavier than a stone.

Hatred.

And it wasn’t hurting Arjun—it was hurting him.

He remembered all the happy moments they had shared.

He remembered how Arjun had always stood by him.

And suddenly, the truth hit him hard.

“I was wrong,” he whispered to himself.

The Decision to Let Go

The next morning, Kiran woke up early. His heart felt heavy, but he knew what he had to do.

He walked towards Arjun’s house.

With every step, his fear grew.

“What if he doesn’t forgive me?”
“What if it’s too late?”

But he kept walking.

When he reached the house, he saw Arjun sitting outside, reading a book.

For a moment, Kiran froze.

Then, gathering all his courage, he called out softly,
“Arjun…”

Arjun looked up, surprised.

There was silence.

The Apology

Kiran walked closer.

“I… I’m sorry,” he said, his voice trembling.
“I shouldn’t have pushed you. I shouldn’t have shouted at you. I let my anger take over.”

Arjun looked at him for a few seconds.

Then he smiled.

“I’ve been waiting to hear that,” he said gently.

Kiran’s eyes filled with tears.

“You’re not angry?” he asked.

“I was hurt,” Arjun replied. “But I didn’t want to hold onto it forever.”

At that moment, something changed.

The weight that Kiran had been carrying for months… disappeared.

The Power of Forgiveness

The two friends sat together again, just like old times.

They talked, laughed, and promised never to let anger come between them again.

Kiran had learned a valuable lesson:

Hatred doesn’t hurt the other person as much as it hurts you.

It slowly takes away your peace, your happiness, and your ability to love.

What Happens When You Hold Onto Hatred?

This story teaches us something very important:

  • Hatred grows when we refuse to forgive
  • It makes small problems feel big
  • It takes away our inner peace
  • It affects our relationships
  • And most importantly, it harms us more than anyone else

Just like holding a heavy stone, the longer you carry hatred, the more painful it becomes.

Why Letting Go Is Important

Letting go doesn’t mean you are weak.

It means you are strong enough to choose peace over anger.

It means you value your happiness more than your ego.

When you forgive:

  • Your heart feels lighter
  • Your mind becomes calm
  • Your relationships improve
  • You become a better person

A Simple Truth to Remember

Ask yourself:

“Is this anger worth holding onto?”

Most of the time, the answer is no.

Life is too short to spend it carrying hatred in your heart.

Moral of the Story

Hatred is like carrying a heavy stone in your heart—the longer you hold it, the more it hurts you. Let go, forgive, and choose peace.
