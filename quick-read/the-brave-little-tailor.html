The Brave Little Tailor

Listen Pause Resume Stop

Once upon a time, in a quaint village nestled at the edge of a dense forest, there lived a humble tailor named Janardana. Janardana was known throughout the village for his skill with a needle and thread, but he longed for adventure beyond the confines of his small shop.

One day, as Janardana sat sewing by the window, he overheard the villagers talking excitedly about a fearsome giant that had been terrorizing the countryside. The giant was said to be the size of a mountain, with a voice like thunder and a temper to match.

Determined to prove his bravery and make a name for himself, Janardana decided to confront the giant and put an end to his reign of terror once and for all. With nothing but his wits and his trusty sewing needle, Janardana set out on his quest.

As he journeyed deeper into the forest, Janardana encountered a series of challenges and obstacles, from treacherous rivers to thickets of thorns. But with courage and determination, he overcame each one, drawing ever closer to the giant's lair.

Finally, after days of travel, Janardana reached the foot of the mountain where the giant dwelled. With his heart pounding in his chest, he approached the giant's cave, his needle clutched tightly in his hand.

To his surprise, the giant was nowhere to be seen. Instead, Janardana found himself face to face with a fearsome band of robbers who had made the cave their hideout. Undaunted, Janardana concocted a clever plan to outwit the robbers and win the day.

Using his sewing needle as a weapon, Janardana pretended to be a powerful magician who could shrink the robbers down to the size of ants. Terrified, the robbers begged for mercy and Janardana promised to spare them if they agreed to leave the village and never return.

With the robbers defeated and the village safe once more, Janardana returned home a hero. The villagers hailed him as the bravest of them all and Janardana's name became known far and wide as the brave little tailor who had outwitted a giant and saved the day.

From that day forth, Janardana lived out his days in peace and contentment, knowing that true bravery lay not in the size of one's sword, but in the strength of one's heart and the cleverness of one's mind.

The moral of the story is that bravery comes in many forms and even the smallest of us can accomplish great things with courage, determination and a little bit of cleverness. Janardana the brave little tailor teaches us that true heroism is not about the size of one's muscles or the sharpness of one's sword, but about the strength of character and the willingness to stand up for what is right.
