How to Care for Your Oral Health as You Age

As you age, maintaining good oral health becomes increasingly important due to changes in your teeth, gums and overall health. Aging can lead to issues such as gum recession, dry mouth and increased risk of decay and periodontal disease. Regular dental check-ups are essential to monitor these changes and address any issues promptly, ensuring your oral health is maintained as you age.

The Role of Vitamin D in Dental Health

Vitamin D plays a crucial role in maintaining strong teeth and overall dental health. It helps the body absorb calcium, which is essential for the development and maintenance of healthy teeth and bones. Adequate vitamin D levels contribute to the formation of a strong tooth enamel, which helps protect against decay and cavities.

How to Treat and Prevent Oral Sores

Oral sores, like canker sores and cold sores, can be painful and annoying. Treating these sores well involves using over-the-counter creams to ease pain and help them heal faster. For canker sores, products with benzocaine or hydrogen peroxide can be helpful. Cold sores, caused by the herpes simplex virus, can be treated with antiviral creams or medications from a doctor.

Consistent care helps manage and reduce the risk of gingivitis and periodontitis, which are common gum conditions that can impact overall health.

How to Maintain a Healthy Smile with Diabetes

Maintaining a healthy smile is especially important for individuals with diabetes, as they are at higher risk for oral health problems such as gum disease and infections. High blood sugar levels can lead to dry mouth, which decreases saliva flow and increases the risk of tooth decay. Managing blood sugar levels through diet, medication and regular check-ups is crucial in preventing these complications and maintaining good oral health.
