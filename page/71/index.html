International Mother Language Day

International Mother Language Day is a worldwide annual observance held on 21st February to promote awareness of linguistic and cultural diversity and to promote multilingualism. First announced by UNESCO on 17 November 1999, it was formally recognized by the United Nations General Assembly with the adoption of UN resolution 56/262 in 2002.

Build self awareness about unconscious bias and why it is needed?

Self-awareness about unconscious bias is crucial for personal and professional development. Unconscious biases are automatic judgments and stereotypes that we hold about people based on characteristics such as race, gender, age, or other social categories. These biases can affect our perceptions, decisions and behaviors in subtle and unintended ways.
